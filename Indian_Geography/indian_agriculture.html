Classification of Crops

According to Seasons

Kharif
  • Sown in June-July when rains first begin (Monsoon crop)
  • Harvested in September-October
  • Requires lot of water and hot weather to grow
  • Example: Rice, Jowar, Bajra, Maize, Cotton, Groundnut, Jute, Sugarcane, Turmeric, Pulses (like Urad Dal) etc

Rabi
  • Sown in October-November
  • Harvested in April-May
  • Requires warm climate for germination of seeds and maturation and cold climate for the growth
  • Example: Wheat, Oat, Gram, Pea, Barley, Potato, Tomato, Onion, Oil seeds (like Rapeseed, Sunflower, Sesame, Mustard) etc

Zaid
  • Grown between March-June between Rabi and Kharif crop seasons
  • Early maturing crops
  • Example: Cucumber, Bitter Gourd, Pumpkin, Watermelon, Muskmelon, Moong Dal etc

Major Crops in India

Rice
  • Type of Crop: Kharif, Rabi
  • Techniques Used: Transplantation technique, Japanese Transplantation technique, new SRI technique
  • Varieties: Aman, Sali, Afghani, Aus, Boro, Palua
  • Temperature: ∼ 24C
  • Rainfall: ∼ 150 cm
  • Soil type: Clay/ Loamy
  • Major Producers: West Bengal, Uttar Pradesh, Andhra Pradesh, Punjab
  • Highest Producer: West Bengal
  • Highest per Hectare Yield: Punjab
  • Research Centres: Cuttack, Odisha
  • Highest Producing Country: China (India 2nd)

Wheat
  • Type of Crop: Rabi
  • Varieties: Sonalika, Kalyansona, Heera
  • Temperature: ∼ 17-20C
  • Rainfall: ∼ 20-100 cm (ideal 75cm)
  • Soil type: Clay/ Loamy, Sandy Loam
  • Major Producers: Uttar Pradesh, Punjab, Madhya Pradesh, Haryana
  • Highest Producer: Uttar Pradesh
  • Highest per Hectare Yield: Punjab
  • Research Centres: Cuttack, Karnal Haryana
  • Highest Producing Country: China (India 2nd)

Cotton
  • Type of Crop: Kharif
  • Varieties: Long, Medium and Short Staple
  • Temperature: ∼ 21C - 30C
  • Rainfall: ∼ 50-100 cm
  • Soil type: Black (Regur)
  • Major Producers: Gujarat, Maharashtra, Andhra Pradesh, Haryana
  • Highest Producer: Gujarat
  • Research Centres: Nagpur Maharashtra
  • Highest Producing Country: China (India 2nd)

Jute
  • Type of Crop: Zaid
  • Varieties: White, Tossa
  • Temperature: ∼ 24C - 35C
  • Rainfall: ∼ 125-200 cm
  • Soil type: Sandy and Clay Loam
  • Major Producers: West Bengal, Bihar, Assam, Andhra Pradesh
  • Highest Producer: West Bengal
  • Research Centres: Kolkata & Nilgunj West Bengal
  • Highest Producing Country: India (but highest exporter is Bangladesh)

Sugercane
  • Type of Crop: Rabi, Kharif
  • Temperature: ∼ 20C - 26C
  • Rainfall: ∼ 75-150 cm
  • Soil type: Clayey Loamy Soil/ Black Cotton Soil/ Red Loamy Soil/ Brown Loamy Soil
  • Major Producers: Uttar Pradesh, Maharashtra, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu
  • Highest Producer: Uttar Pradesh
  • Highest Per Hectre Yield : Tamil Nadu (South Indian Sugercane have higher sugar content)
  • Research Centres: Lucknow
  • Highest Producing Country: Brazil

Tea
  • Temperature: ∼ 20C - 30C (Grows faster in light shade)
  • Rainfall: ∼ 150-300 cm
  • Soil type: Loamy soil which is acidic in nature and rich in organic matter
  • Major Producers: Assam, Darjeeling (West Bengal), Meghalaya, Kerala
  • Highest Producer: Assam
  • Research Centres: Tocklai Assam
  • Highest Producing Country: China

Coffee
  • Variety: Arabica, Robusta
  • Temperature: ∼ 16C - 28C (Needs two tier of shade)
  • Rainfall: ∼ 150-250 cm
  • Soil type: Well drained forest loam
  • Major Producers: Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Kerala, Andhra Pradesh
  • Highest Producer: Karnataka
  • Research Centres: Kasharagarh Kerala, Chikmagalur Karnataka
  • Highest Producing Country: Brazil

Agricultural Revolutions

Green Revolution
  • Refers to a period when Indian agriculture was converted into an industrial system due to the adoption of modern methods and technology such as the use of High yielding variety (HYV) seeds and modern farming methods
  • Term coined by William S Gadd of the USAID in 1968
  • Started in Mexico under Norman E Borlaug and Hassan
  • Occured in India during the 3rd 5 year plan (1961-1966)

Second Green Revolution
  • Green revolution in India was only limited to a few states
  • Chairman of National Commission on Farmer has called for a Second Green Revolution
  • This was stressed upon in the 11th 5year plan

White Revolution
  • Operation Flood initiative taken by National Dairy Development Board in 1970
  • Occured during the 4th 5year plan (1969-74)
  • Verghese Kurien called the Father of White Revolution
  • Three Stages of Operation Flood
    1. Stage I (1970-79)
    2. Stage II (1980-85)
    3. Stage III (1985-94)

Yellow Revolution
  • Relted to achieving self sufficiency in production of Edible oil
  • Occured during the 6th 5year plan (1980-85)
  • Directed by Sam Pitroda

Blue Revolution
  • Refers to Scientific cultivation of Fishes
  • Arup Krishnan is called the Father of Blue Revolution

Grey Revolution
  • Grey revolution is associated with increased fertilizer production

Pink Revolution
  • Pink revolution is associated with increased Shrimp (Red Meat), Pharmaceuticals and onion production

Black Revolution
  • Black revolution is associated with increased minerals (Crude Oil) production
