Geological Structure of India

The Archean Formations

General Information
  • Also known as Pre-Cambrian period
  • Timescale from Formation of Earth (4.6 billion year ago) to begining of the Cambrian period (570 million year ago)
  • Unfossiliferous rocks, devoid of any sediments
  • Archean rocks are repositories of mineral wealth such as iron-ore, copper, manganese, mica, gold and silver

Classification

Bengal Gneiss
  • First identified in Midnapore district of West Bengal
  • Highly foliated
  • Streching over Manbhum, Hazaribagh of Jharkhand, Nellore of Andhra Pradesh and Salem of Tamil Nadu
  • Also occur in Meghalaya plateaue and Mikir hills

Bundelkhand Gneiss
  • Massive granitoid, conspicuously criss-crossed, characterized by quartz veins
  • Occurs in Bundelkhand (UP), Bahgelkhand (MP), Maharashtra, Rajasthan, Andra Pradesh and Tamilnadu
  • Streching over Manbhum, Hazaribagh of Jharkhand, Nellore of Andhra Pradesh and Salem of Tamil Nadu

Nilgiri Gneiss
  • Massive eruptive bluish-grey to dark-colored gneiss
  • Belonging to the Charnokite series (in honor of Job Charnock)
  • Occurs in South Arcot, Palini Hills, Shevaroy and Nilgiri hills in Tamilnadu, Nellore in Andhra Pradesh, Balasore in Odisha as well as Malabar, Jharkhand, Chattishgarh, Kerala and the Aravallis
