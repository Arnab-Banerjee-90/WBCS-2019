Physiographic Divisions of India

Elevated Peninsular Region

Relief Map

Introduction
  • Largest physiographic division, with an area of 16 lakh sq km
  • General Elevation between 600-900 meters
  • Bounded by
    • Aravalli in the NW
    • Hazaribagh and Rajmahal in the NE
    • Western Ghats (Sahayadra mountains) in the W
    • Eastern Ghats in the E
  • Anai-Mudi of the Nilgiri is the highest peak 2695 m

Central Highlands

The Aravalli Range
  • Runs from NE to SW from Delhi to Palanpur for about 800 km
  • One of the oldest folded mountains of the world
  • Mainly composed of quartz, gniess and schists of Precambrian age
  • North-West of Udaipur it is known as Jarga Hills
  • Highest peak is Guru Sikhar 1722 m
  • The Goranghat Pass seperates Guru Sikhar from Mt. Abu
  • The Great Boundary Fault seperates Aravalli from the Vindhyas

The Malwa Plateau
  • Bounded by Aravallis to the NW, Bundelkhand plateau to the E and Vindhya to the S
  • Has 2 Drainage systems
    • Towards the Arabian Sea Narmada and Mahi
    • Towards the Bay of Bengal Chambal, Betwa, Ken

The Bundelkhand Plateau
  • Bounded by Yamuna river in the N, Vindhya in the S, and Malwa plateau to the W
  • Streches over parts of UP, MP
  • Mainly composed of Granite

The Vindhya Range
  • Extended from West-Jobat (Gujarat) to East-Sararam (Bihar), almost prarallal to the Narmada valley for about 1200 km
  • Composed mainly of Sandstone
  • Sadbhawna Shikhar/Kalumar Peak is the higheat peak of the Vindhyas, only 750 m
  • Eastern parts of Vindhya is known as Kaimur
  • Creates a division between rivers of North and South India

Narmada Valley
  • Situated to the south of the Vindhya
  • Created due to parallel fault lines
  • Denotes the bounadry of the Central Highlands

Eastern Highlands

Baghelkhand Plateau
  • Situated to the South of Son river
  • Bagelkhand is surrounded by the Indo-Gangetic plains in NE, Bundelkhand in W and the Vindhya range S and Chotanagpur plateau in E
  • Composed mainly of Granite of Vindhyan age

Chotanagpur Plateau
  • Sprawls over parts of West Bengal, Jharkhand, Chattisgarh, Odisha and North East of Andhra Pradesh
  • Pareshnath is its highest peak 1366 m
  • Western regions of the plateau are the highest regions, known as Pat Lands
  • Composed of Archean granite, gniess rocks with patches of Dharwar, Damuda formations and lava flow of the Cretaceous period
  • Barakar, Damodar, Subarnarekha drains the Chotanagpur plateau

Mahanadi Basin & Chattisgarh Plains
  • Mahanadi basin sprawls over Raipur, Bilaspur, Durg and Rajgarh
  • Drained by Mahanadi and its tributaries i.e Seonath, Mand, Ib, Tel etc
  • Chattisgarh plains are borderd by series of hills and plateaus
  • The Gondwana formations are rich in bituminous coal
  • The Rajhara hills contain Dharwarian rocks rich in haematite iron ore

Garhjat hills
  • Known as Odisha highlands
  • Bordered by Chotanagpur plateau in N, Mahanadi basin in the W
  • Mainly composed of Archean rocks

Dandakaranya
  • It is an undulating plateau
  • Sprawls over parts of Odisha, Chattisgarh, Andhra Pradesh
  • Its Abujhmar hills provide one of the richest iron ore deposits in the Bailadila range
  • Drained by Tel and Udanti tributaries of Godavari

Southern Highlands

Satpura Mahadav and Mahakal
  • A series of seven ranges
  • Lies to the South of the Vindhyas and in between Narmada and Tapi rivers
  • Dhupgarh is its highest peakof the Satpura 1350 m
  • Amarkantak is the highest peak of the Mahakal
  • Panchmari is the highest peak of the Mahadev
  • The Maikal range forms a link between the Vindhya and Satpuras

Nilgiri Range/Knot
  • Eastern and Western Ghats merge with the Nilgiri mountain range
  • Dodabetta is the highest peak of the Nilgiris
  • Annmalai range merges from the north
  • Anaimudi peak of the Annamalai is the highest peak of peninsular India
  • Cardamom (Elaimalai) merges from the south
  • Palni merges from the North-East

Eastern Ghats (Malayadri)
  • Forms the Eastern boundary of the Deccan plateau
  • Average height of 600m
  • Between Mahanadi and Godavari the average height is 1100m
  • Jindhagada is the highest peak
  • Aroya-Konda, Dewodi-Munda, Singa-Raju, and Nimalgiri are other important peaks

Western Ghats (Sahayadri)
  • Lies to the west along the coast of the Arabian sea
  • Length of 1600km along North-South
  • From West to East it's height diminishes in steps and it merges with the plateaus
  • Impotant peaks are Kudermukh, Kalsubai, Mahabaleshwar, Salher
  • Bhor Ghat pass joins Mumbai with Pune
  • Goran Ghat pass lies to the south of Aount Abu joins Udaipur with Sirohi and Jalore
  • Haldighat pass located 40km from Udaipur joins Rajasmand and Pali districts
  • Jog Falls on the Sharavati river
  • Pal Ghat pass joins Coimbatore with Kochi and Kozhikode
  • Thal Ghat pass joins Mumbai with Nasik (NH-3)

Maharashtra Plateau (Deccan Trap)
  • Gondwana formations of the Cretaceous age composed of Basalt
  • Covers the entire state of Maharashtra
  • Basaltic thickness around 3km in western parts
  • Thickness diminishes towards the east in steps
  • The lava soil is black in color known as Black-Cotton soil or Regur
  • The soil is somewhat Salty in nature

Meghalaya Plateau and Mikir Hills
  • Consists of Garo, Khasi, Jayantia hills and the outlying Mikir and Rengma hills
  • Seperated from southern plateau by Malda Gap
  • Shilliong Peak is the highest elevation in this plateau 1823 m
  • Nokrek is the highest peak of the Garo hills
  • Southern range of the Mikir hills are known as Rengma hills
  • This plateau is drained by Dhansiri and Jamuna rivers

Karantaka Plateau
  • Lies to the south of the Deccan traps
  • Average height of 600-900 m, the soil is Reddish in color
  • The highest peak (1913 m) is at Mulangiri in Baba Budan Hills in Chikmaglur district.
  • The plateau is divided into two parts called Malnad and Maidan
  • The Malnad in Kannada means hill country. It is dissected into deep valleys covered with dense forests
  • The Maidan on the other hand is formed of rolling plain with low granite hills.

Telengana Plateau
  • Lies to the East of Maharashtra and Karnataka plateaus
  • The Telangana plateau consists of Archaean gneisses
  • Itâ€™s average elevation is 500-600 m.
  • The region is drained by three river systems, the Godavari, the Krishna and the Penneru.
  • The southern part is higher than its northern counterpart.
  • The entire plateau is divided into Ghats and the Peneplains (a vast featureless, undulating plain which the last stage of deposition process)

The Himalayas

Relief Map

Physiographical Division

The Shiwaliks
  • The Southernmost range
  • The Siwaliks Ranges are in the outer zone
  • The elevation is from 600 to 1500m
  • This is a discontinuous range, elongated to 2400km, including 90km long Gorges of the Tista and the Raidak
  • It includes the Jammu Hills
  • Notable valleys are Dehra Dun, Kotah, Patli, Kothri, Chumbi, and Kyarda.

The Himachals
  • The Himachal Ranges are called as the Lesser Himalayas
  • The elevation ranges from 1000 to 4500m
  • Width is 60-80km
  • It includes parallel ranges of Nepal and Punjab
  • Parallel valleys in between the Shiwaliks and the Himachals are called Duns
  • Notable Ranges are Pirpanjal, Dhauladhar, Nag Tiba, Mussourie, Mahabharat
  • The world famous hill stations like Shimla, Chail, Ranikhet, Chakrata, Mussourie, Nainital , Almora and Darjeeling are all situated in this range.

The Himadris
  • The MCT (Main Central Thrust) seperates the Greater from the Lsesser himalayas
  • The Himadri ranges are called as the Greater Himalays
  • The elevation is from 4500 to 6000m.
  • This range houses the highest peaks of the Himalayas. The average height is 6100m
  • Most of the lofty peaks such as Mount Everest (8848m), Kanchenjunga (8598m), Makalu (8481m), Dhulagiri (8172m), Mansalu (8152m) reside in the Himadris
  • To the north, the Himadris have merged into the Tibetan plateau of the Trans-Himalayas

Trans Himalayas
  • This is a vast Plateau region
  • This is about 40km wide and 965km long
  • It consists of Ladakh Karakoram and Kailash ranges from South to North
  • The most notable peak is K2, the second highest peak in the world having an altitude of 8,611 m.

Longitudinal Division
  • Proposed by S Burrard

Kashmir Himalayas
  • Covers 350000 sqkm area of Kashmir
  • It includes Karakoram & Laddakh Plateau of the Trans Himalayas, Kashmir valley and Pir Panjal of the Himachals
  • Pir panjal lying 100 km south of Srinagar has seperated Kashmir valley from the rest of India
  • Karewa deposit consisting of sand, clay and silt is a special feature of the Kashmir valley
  • Karewas are mainly devoted to the cultivation of saffron, peach, apricot, apple, almonds etc.

Himachal Himalayas
  • Mostly located in Himachal Pradesh, covering area of 56000 sqkm
  • The Shiwaliks lie in the southernmost part
  • North of the Shiwaliks there are Dhawladhar and Nagtibba ranges of the Himachals
  • Further to the north there lies the Pir Panjal (Himachals) and the Zaskar (Trans Himalayas) ranges
  • Rohtang Pass lies in this region
  • Main valleys of this region are Kulu, Spiti, Lahul
  • Shimla, Dalhousie, Dharamshala etc Hill stations are within this region

Kumayun Himalayas
  • Located entirely within the state of Uttarakhand, between Satjul and Kali rivers
  • Covers ans area of 46000 sqkm
  • Shiwalik, Himachal and Himadri are well represented here
  • Dehra Dun is the largest valley of this region
  • Many high altitude lakes known as Tals can be seen
  • Nainital, Bhimtal, Sattal etc are well known
  • Main peaks of this region include Nandadevi, Trishul, Chaukhamba, Dungiri, Gangotri etc
  • Gangotri and Yamunotri glaciers are also located in this region

Middle/Nepal Himalayas
  • From the west of Kali river to Singalila range lies the Middle Himalayas
  • It is totally situated in Nepal
  • Mount Everest, Makalu, Annupurna, Dhaulagiri etc peaks lie here

Sikkim/Darjeeling Himalayas
  • Situated in between Singalila and Dongkiala ranges (West to East)
  • Singalila has separated Darjeeling from Nepal
  • Kanchenjungha is the highest peak of this region and the 3rd highest peak of the world (8598m)
  • Sandakfu, Falut, Sabagram are the main peaks of Singalila
  • Nathula, Zelepla are the main passes

Purvachals
  • Lie between Teesta and Brahmaputra rivers
  • Spread across 67000 sqkm area of Arunachal Pradesh and Bhutan
  • Shiwalik, Himachal and Himadri are well represented in this region
  • Namchabarwa is the highest peak of this region (7756 m)
  • Many valleys are present here between the mountians, Kamla is the most notable
  • Bomdi-La, Bom-La, Tunga, Domla are the main passes
  • On the Southers borders of Arunachal Pradesh, Himallays take a southern turn
  • Passing through Nagaland, Manipur, Tripura and Mizoram
  • The main hills are Patkai-Bum (AP), Naga hills (NGL), Blue Mountains (MZR)
  • Saramati is the highest peak of the Naga hills
  • On the border of Nagaland and Myanmar lies the Arakanyoma hills

Coastal Plains

Coastline Map

Western Coastal Plains

Gujarat Coastal Plains
Rann
  • Salt marsh located in the north of Gujarat state
  • It is an extensive tract of naked tidal mudflats
  • Northern part is called the Great Rann
  • Eastern part is called the Little Rann
  • In the rainy season this region gets inundated by Luni, and the Gulf of Kachh
Gulf Of Kachh
  • From Okha to Kandla a part of Arabian sea has penetrated into Gujarat, it is known as the Gulf of Kachh
Kathiwar Peninsula
  • Surrounded by sea on three sides by Arabian sea, Gulf of Khambat, Gulf Kachh
  • Low wetlands in the north-east of this reagion known as Nal
  • Girnar is the highest peak
Gulf of Khambat
  • It is a part of Arabian sea which has penetrated inland from Bhavangar to Baroda

Konkan Coast
  • Lies to the West of the Western ghats
  • Streches from Surat to Goa for about 530km
  • This cost is very narrow and submerged
  • Characterized by sandy beaches, mud-flats, lagoons and alluvial tracts among rivers
  • Vaitarani, Amba, Ulhas are the main rivers which flow through this region

Karnataka Coast
  • Streches from the south of Konkan coast to the entire coastline of Karnataka
  • Length 525km width 8-25km
  • Mostly narrow, but widens up to 70km mear Mangalore
  • Netravati, Saravati, Kali are the main rivers flowing in this region

Malabar Coast
  • Streches from the South of Karnataka all the way to Kanyakumari
  • Length 550km width 20-100km
  • This Coastline is wider than the previous coastlines of the West coast
  • Many coastal sand-dune, lakes, back-waters, lagoons, etc can be ssen here
  • The coastal beaches and sand-dunes are known as Theris
  • These lakes are known as Kayal and Teri
  • Among these the 80km long Vembanad Kayal and the Ashtamudi is famous (both in Kerala)

Eastern Coastal Plains

North Cicar Coast
  • Spreads from Subernarekha to the East of Krishna river delta
Odisah/Utkal Coast
  • Streches from Subarnarekha to Chilka lake
  • Made up of the deltas of Mahanadi, Vaitarani, Brahmani
  • To the south of the Mahanadi delta lies the Chilka Lake
Andhra Coast
  • Spread from Chilka Lake to Krishna river Delta
  • Kolar lake (Kulleru) is situated between Godavari and Krishna deltas
  • The Deltas of Krishna and Godavari have extented far Westward into the mainland almost 280km

Coromondal Coast
  • Streching from the South of Krishna river Delta to Kanyakumari
  • The region of the coastline form Pulicat lake to Kanyakumari is also called Tamilnadu coast
  • In the Tamilnadu coast there is present the large Kaveri river delta extending westward inland for 100km
  • Thavanjore in Tamilnadu coast is called the Grain basket of South India
  • Gulf of Mannar has seperated Srilanka from Tamilnadu

Indian Desert Region

Relief Map

Introduction
  • Located in between the Aravalli range and the Satluj and Indus plains
  • Spread across 269000 sqkm, out of which 196747 sqkm is within Indian territory
  • Average height is 150 m
  • Height Gradually diminishes from the Aralvallis in the south east(350) to the Pakistan plains in the north west (150)
  • In the lower areas and to the north of Jaisalmeer, some salt lakes or Ranns can be seen
  • Lake Sambar is the largest lake of this region

Reflief Features

Bagar
  • The foot area of the Aravallis i.e the easternmost region of the Thar desert is known as Bagar
  • Almost all of this region is covered in grass and some agriculture is seen
  • Rajasthan Canal has helped the spread of agriculture

Rohi
  • Some rivers have orginated from the western slopes of the Aravallis
  • These rivers have dried up and have merged into the desert region
  • But alluvial deposits on both sides of these rivers, makes the basins fertile
  • These fertile lands are known as Rohi

Little Desert Region
  • Located to the immediate west of the Rohi region
  • This area marks the true begining of the desert

    • Stony region or Hamada
    • Located to the north of the Little Desert region
    • This region is made up of sandy soft rock deposits

    Sandy Region
    • Located to the West of the stony region
    • This region is the great Indian Thar desert, which has crossed over into Pakistan
    • This region is also known as Marusthali
    Dhrian
    • Some sand dunes change location due to the wind, these moving sand dunes are known as Dhrian or Tibba
    Dhand
    • Sometimes in between two parallel sand dunes there exists longitudinal lakes
    • These lakes remain dry for most of the year
    • These lakes are known locally as Dhand

    Great Indian Plains

    Plains Regions on the Map

    Introduction
    • Located in Between the northern Himalayas and the Peninsular Plateau region
    • The river basin of Ganges, Indus and Brahmaputra have created this great planar region
    • It is 2400km in length and 240-320km wide
    • Height of this region varies from 3-4m near the bay of Bengal to 215m near Delhi and 235m in Punjab

    Types of Soils

    Bhabar
    • A region south of the Lower Himalayas and the Shiwalik Hills
    • It is the alluvial apron of sediments washed down from the Siwaliks along the northern edge of the Indo-Gangetic Plain
    • Covers a majority of Uttarakhand

    Tarai
    • A belt of marshy jungle lying between the lower foothills of the Himalayas and the plains
    • Terai spreads from the Yamuna River eastward across Himachal Pradesh, Haryana, Uttarakhand, Uttar Pradesh and Bihar

    Khadar and Bet
    • The alluvial soil found in the large part of North India is called Khadar
    • It is prone to flooding, very fertile in nature
    • This soil covers majority of the Middle and lower Gangetic plains

    Bhangar
    • Bangar/Bhangar areas are beyond floodplains that lie more upland, and consist of older alluvial soil. Bangar area are less prone to flooding but are usually more sandy and less fertile as well

    Division of the Plains

    Punjab Plains
    • It orginates due to alluvial deposits from the tributaries of Indus, mainly Satluj
    • Spread across the western bank of the Yamunas, in the states of Punjab, Delhi, Haryana, parts of Rajasthan and Uttarpradesh

    Gangetic Plains
    Upper Gangetic Plain
    • Includes Ganga-Yamuna Doab, Rohilkhand division and parts of the Agra division
    • Yamuna lies to its west and the Shiwaliks to the north
    • Elevation varies from 100-300m
    • Traversed by Kali and Sharda rivers in addition to Ganga and Yamuna
    • A unique feature is the presence of Bhur which is an undulating aeolian sandy deposit
    • Above mentioned Bhur is dedicated to the production of Sugercane, rice, wheat, maize, pulses etc.
    Middle Gangetic Plains
    • Sprawling over an area of 144000sqkm, includes, Eastern and central UttarPradesh and Bihar upto Muzaffarpur and Patna
    • Bounded by the Shiwaliks along the Indo-Nepal border
    • Homogeneous, thick alluvial deposit is abundant
    • Rivers often change their course in this region
    • Important Left hand tributaries of Ganges in the middle Gangetic plains are Gandak, Kosi
    • Important Right hand tributary of Ganges is Son
    Lower Gangetic Plains
    • Extends from Patna in the West, the foor of the Himalays in the north to the Bay of Bangla in the South
    • Bordered by Assam and Bangladesh in the east and Chotonagpur plateau in the West
    • This region can be divided into 4 parts
    Tarai and Dooars
    • Eastern part of the plain, drained by Teesta, Jaldhaka, Sankosh
    • Right side of Teesta is called Tarai
    • Left side of Teesta is called Dooars
    • Covers the northern region of Coochbihar, Jalpaiguri districts of West Bengal
    • Fertility of this plains is comparatively low
    Gangetic Delta
    • It is a part of the largest river delta of Bhagirathi-Hooghly
    • Ganges, Ajay, Damodar, Mayurakkhi etc drain thsi region
    • Fertility of this region is very high
    • This delta region is classified into three regions
        • Dying Delta
      • This part lies in the district of Nadia, Murshidabad
      • Rivers of this regions such as Jalangi, Churni, Ichhamati are seperated from Ganges and no loger takes part in delta creation
        Well Formed Delta
      • This part lies in the district of Burdwan, Medinipur, Hooghly and Howrah
      • Formed from the alluvial deposits of river from the Chotanagpur plateau
      • The soil here is hard in nature
        Active Delta
      • This part lies in the district of North and South 24 Parganas
      • The world's largest mangrove forest can be seen here
      • The soil of this region is salty
    The Barendras
    • Lies to the South of the Tarai and Dooars plains
    • Extends upto the left bank of the Ganges
    • Soil of the Northern part of this region is relatively fertile
    • Whereas the soil of the sputhern part is lateraite soil and grainy and has less fertility
    The Rahr
    • Lies between the delta region and the Chotanagpur plateau
    • Drained by Damodar, Subarnarekha
    • Soil is laterite in nature and reddish in color, slightly alkaline, and not mush fertile

    Plains of Brahmaputra
    Map
    • Brahmaputra valley is situated to the east of the Ganges plains
    • Made up of alluvial deposits from Brahmaputra and its many tributaries
    • It is 700km long and has a width of 80km
    • The soil of this region is very fertile
    • Due to low gradient the Brahmaputra is a highly braided, many islands can be seen in this region
    • Majuli is India's largest (2nd largest in the world) river delta can be seen here (Second to Marajo island of the Amazon)
