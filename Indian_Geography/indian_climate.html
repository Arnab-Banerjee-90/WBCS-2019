    La-Nino
    • Female Counterpart of El-Nino
    • Climate pattern that describes the cooling of surface ocean waters along the tropical west coast of South America
    • It is a harbinger of heavy moonsoon showers in India

    ENSO
    • El-Nino southern Oscillation
    • Discovered by British climatologist Gilbert Walker
    • Whenever the surface level pressure is high over the Indian ocean, there is low pressure over the Pacific ocean and vice versa, this inter-relation of high and low pressure over the Pacific and the Indian ocean is called Southern Oscillation
    • Affects the climate of much of the tropics and subtropics

    MONEX
    • The effects of monsoons in different parts of the world on the Earth's atmosphere were studied by MONEX, India's Monsoon Experiment program

    Cloud Burst
    • Excessive rainfall over 100mm in 1 hour, In India it occurs in the Western Ghats and the Himalayas
    • Wet Day is referred to as a day with 1mm rainfall in 24 hours
    • Rainy Day is referred to as a day with minimum of 0.25mm rainfall in 24 hours

    ITCZ
    • The Inter Tropical Convergence Zone, or ITCZ, is a belt of low pressure which circles the Earth generally near the equator where the trade winds of the Northern and Southern Hemispheres come together

    Western Disturbance
    • A Western Disturbance is an extratropical storm originating in the Mediterranean region that brings sudden winter rain to the northwestern parts of the Indian subcontinent
    • Western Disturbances are important for the development of the Rabi crop, which includes the locally important staple wheat

Climatic Regions of India
  • Trewartha's Classification
  • Derived from Kopen's Classification
  • Divides India into 4 major climatic regions A,B,C and H
  • A refers to the tropical rainy climate with high temperature
  • B refers to dry climate with high temperatute and little rainfall
  • C refers to dry winters with low temperatures ranging from 10-18° celcius
  • H refers to mointainous climate
  • A,B types are furthur sub-divided
Region Location Features
Tropical Rain Forests (Am) Western Ghats, West Coastal region, Parts of Assam and Tripura High tempreature throughout the year, more than 300cm/year rainfall
Tropical Savannah (Aw) Majority of Peninsular India with exception of the leeward side of Western Ghats Dry winter, 76-150cm rainfall
Tropical Semi-Arrid Steppe (Bs) Rainshadow belt running from Central Maharashtra to Tamilnadu Low Rainfall varies from 38cm-80cm and temperature from 20-30°
Tropical and Sub tropical Steppes (Bsh) Punjab, Kachh region and Haryana Dry winter Temperature between 12-35° celcius
Tropical Desert Climate (Bwh) Western Rajasthan, parts of Kachchh Scanty rainfall, very high temperature
Humid Sub-tropical (Caw) South of Himalayas Mild winters and very hot summers
Mountain Climate (H) Jammu Kashmir, Himachal Pradesh (above 6000m) Majority of the year is Snow Capped, 0-10 degree celcius average tempareture
