Indian Vegetation

Classification of Forests

Map of Natural Vegetation

Tropical Evergreen (16.2%)

Distribution
  • Rainy solpes of Western Ghats
  • NE India except Arunachal Pradesh
  • Anadaman & Nicobar Islands
  • Eastern parts of Odisha and West Bengal

Climatic Conditions
  • Rainfall>200cm
  • Relative Humidity>70%
  • Average Temperature 24° Celsius
  • Hot and humid

Characteristics
  • Height of trees 40-60m
  • Leaves are dark green and broad

Species
  • Mahogany,Mahua,Bamboo,Cone,Ironwood,Kadam,Rubber etc

Tropical Moist Decidous (37%)

Distribution
  • Eastern parts of Western Ghats
  • NE Part of the peninsula
  • Middle and lower Gangetic plains
  • Foothills of the himalayas in Bhabar and Tarai regions

Climatic Conditions
  • Rainfall 100-200cm
  • Moderate Temperature

Characteristics
  • Height of trees 30-40m
  • Shed leaves in summer time due to deficiency of water

Species
  • Sal, Teak, Arjun, Mulberry, Kusum, Sandalwood, Haldi, Khair, Mango etc

Tropical Dry Decidous (28.6%)

Distribution
  • Large parts of Maharashtra, Telengana, Andhra Pradesh and Tamilnadu
  • Few parts of Punjab, Haryana, and Eastern parts of Rajasthan
  • Northern and Western parts of Madhya Pradesh
  • Southern parts of Uttar Pradesh

Climatic Conditions
  • Rainfall 50-100cm
  • Moderate Humidity

Characteristics
  • Height of trees 6-15m
  • Roots are thick and long

Species
  • Teak, Sal, Bamboo, Mango, Acasia, Neem etc

Dry/Arid Forests

Distribution
  • Rajasthan and adjoining arear of Punjab, Haryana, Gujarat
  • Rainshadow areas of peninsular India

Climatic Conditions
  • Rainfall<200cm
  • Relative Humidity is less

Characteristics
  • Thorny vegetation
  • Long roots and small leaves

Species
  • Castus, thorny bushes, Babool, Date palm, Acacia, Euphorbias etc

Mountanous or Himalayan

Distribution
  • In the Himalayan regions

Climatic Conditions
  • Due to less temperature Himalayn forests all the varieties of the world except equatorial forests

Characteristics
  • Each vegetation belt occurs at a height of more than 300m

Species
  • Sal, Taek, Deodar, Olive, Chestnut, Conifers, Spruce, Lirch etc

Important Facts
  • Natural forest coverage in India is 21.54% which is less than the world average of 30.4%
  • Riparian Trees are referred to the vegetation surrounding the river banks
  • Sal Trees ocuurs maximum in Madhya Pradesh
  • Sandlewood Trees occur maximum in Karnataka
  • Champain has divided the natural vegetation of India into 116 types furthur subdivided into 113 sub sections
