Citizenship Act, 1955

Acquisition Of Citizenship

By Birth
  • Born in India between 26th Jan 1950 - 1st July 1987
  • Born in India after 1st July 1987,and either or both of the parents must be a citizen of India at the time of birth
  • Born in India after 3rd December 2004,and both of the parents must be a citizen of India at the time of birth

By Descent
  • A person born outside India between 26th Jan 1950 - 10th Dec 1992 is citizen by descent if his/her father was a citizen of India at the time of birth
  • A person born outside India after 10th Dec 1992 is citizen by descent if either of his/her parents was a citizen of India at the time of birth
  • From 3rd Dec 2004 onwards a person born outside India will not be an Indian citizen by descent unless his/her birth is registered at an Indian consulate within one year form the date of birth

By Registration
  • Persons of Indian origin, residing in India for 7 years
  • Women who are or have been married to an Indian citizen
  • Minor children of persons who are citizens of India

By Naturalization
  • Person of full age and capacity
  • Must not be a citizen of such a counrty where Indian citizens are prohibited from becoming citizens by naturalization
  • He has renounced the citizenship of the other counrty
  • Resided in India or has been in Government service for 12 months
  • Must take oath of allegiance to the Indian Constitution

By Incorporation of territory
  • If a territory becomes a part of India, after popular verdict the Government of India shall specify the person of that territory to be citizens of India

Loss of CItizenship

Renounciation
  • Voluntary act
  • Person acquires citizenship of other country and voluntarily gives up Indian citizenship

Termination
  • Operation of law
  • When a person becomes a citizen of any other country he automatically loses Indian citizenship

Deprivation
  • Compulsory termination of citizenship obtained through Registration or Naturalization
  • Charges of using fradulent means to acquire citizenship
