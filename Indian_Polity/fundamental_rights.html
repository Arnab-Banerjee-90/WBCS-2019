Fundamental Rights

Origins
  • Inspired from the American Constituion (Bill of Rights)
  • First put forward by the Nehru report (1928)

Fundamental Rights At A Glance
Category Articles
General
  • Article 12 → Definition Of State
  • Article 13 → Laws inconsistent with or in derogation of the Fundamental Rights
Right to Equality
  • Article 14 → Equality before Law
  • Article 15 → Prohibition of discrimination only on the grounds of race, religion, caste, sex and birthplace
  • Article 16 → Equal oppertunity in matters of public employment
  • Article 17 → Abolition Of Untouchability
  • Article 18 → Abolition of titles except Academic and military
Right to Freedom
  • Article 19 → Protection of Six Rights
    1. Freedom Of Speech
    2. Freedom of assembly
    3. Right to forming associations
    4. Freedom of movement
    5. Freedom of residence
    6. Freedom of profession
    7. Right to acquire, hold and dispose of property (Removed by 44th Ammendemt Act of 1978)
  • Article 20 → Protection in respect of conviction of offences
    1. No ex-post-facto law
    2. No double jeopardy
    3. No self-incrimination
  • Article 21 → Protection of Life and Personal Liberty (34 rights)
  • Article 21 A → Right to education (Added by 86th Ammendment of 2002)
  • Article 22 → Protection against arrest and detention in certain cases
Right against exploitation
  • Article 23 → Prohibition of human trafficing and forced labour
  • Article 24 → Prohibition of employment of children in factories etc.
Right to freedom of religion
  • Article 25 → Freedom of conscience, free profession, practice and propagation of any religion. It does not give the right to convert
  • Article 26 → Freedom to manage religious affairs
  • Article 27 → Freedom from payment of taxes for promotion of a particular religion
  • Article 28 → Freedom as to attendance at religious instructions/worship in certain institution
Cultural and Educational Rights
  • Article 29 → Protection of interests of minorities
  • Article 30 → Right of minorities to establish and administer educational institutions
Right to Constitutional Remedy
  • Article 32
    • Called "The Heart and Soul the Constitution" by B.R Ambedkar
    • To enforce Fundamental Rights, the Supreme Court is empowered under this article to issue Writs

Forms Of Writs

Habeas Corpus
  • Literally means 'to have the body of'
  • Issued to protect personal liberty of individual against arbitrary actions of both state and individual

Mandamus
  • Literally means 'command'
  • Issued against a public authority or an officer and inferior courts for compelling the authority to do a particular work, which the authority ought to do but has not done

Prohibition
  • Issued by the Higher courts to the lower courtsor quasi-judicial bodies when the latter has exceeded their judicial authority

Certiorari
  • Literally means 'to be certified'
  • Issued to quash the order of alower court or tribunal in excess of its jurisdiction

Quo-Warranto
  • Literally means 'by what authority'
  • Issued to ensure that the person holding any public office is really qualified to do so

Absolute Rights
  • Only Article 17 and Article 24 are Absolute rights

Non Fundamental Rights
  • Article 265 → No tax shall be collected/levied except by authority of law
  • Article 300 → A Right to Property
  • Article 301 → Freedom of trade, commerce and intercourse
  • Article 326 → Right to vote

Ammendability Of FR
  • Shankari Prasad Case (1951) the Supreme Court held that the Parliament can ammend any of the provisions of the constitution including Fundamental Rights under Article 368
  • Golaknath Case (1967) the Supreme Court reversed earlier stand, and claimed that Fundamental Rights are transcendental and cannot be subjected to ammendment
  • Kesavananda Bharati case (1973) the Supreme Court came up with a balanced approach

Suspension Of FR
  • Under Article 358, the Fundamental rights of Article 19 are automatically suspended in case of a National Emergency
  • 44th Ammendment Act (1978) Prohibits the suspension of Article 20, Article 21 even during National Emergency
