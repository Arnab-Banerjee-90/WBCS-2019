The Union Executive

The President

The First Citizen

Article 52
  • There shall be a president of India

Article 53(I)
  • The executive power of the Union shall be vested in the President
  • It shall be exercised by him either directly or through officers subordinate to him in accordance with this Constitution.

Election

Article 54
  • Not directly elected
  • Elected by an Electoral College whose members are directly elected
  • Elected members of both houses of parliament
  • Elected members of state legislative assemblies
  • Elected members of Legislative assemblies of Delhi and Puducherry
  • Nominated members of both houses of parliament and state legislature dont participate

Article 55
  • Unimormity in the scale of representation of different states
  • Value of MLA vote → (Total Population of the state/Total Number of Elected members in the State Legislative Assembly) * 1/1000
  • Value of MP vote → (Total value of MLA votes/Total Number of Elected members in the Parliament)
  • In accordance with the system of Proportional representation by means of the Single transferable vote
  • Voting shall be done using Secret Ballot

Article 57
  • President is eligible for re-election

Article 58
  • Candidate must be Citizen of India
  • Of age 35 years and above
  • Qualified to become member of the Lok Sabha
  • Shall not be eligible if candidate holds any office of profit under the government of India

Article 71
  • All doubts and disputes arising out of or in connection with the election of a Presidentshall be inquired into and decided by the Supreme Court whose decision shall be final

Term of Office

Article 56
  • The President shall hold office for a term of five years from the date on which he enters upon his office
  • Even after term has ended he/she will conttinue to be President untill a new President is sworn in
  • Resignation has to be addressed to Vice-Preseident, who will then communicate to the Speaker of Lok Sabha

Oath or Affirmation

Article 60
  • Oath or affirmation of office is administered by Chief Justice of India and in his absense the seniormost judge of the supreme court

Conditions and Priviledges of Office

Article 59
  • Personal immunity from legal liabilities and criminal acts, civil proceedings can be instituted after 2 months notice
  • His emoluments and allowance are determined by the parliament
  • Emoluments and allowances cannot be diminished during his term of office

Impeachment

Article 61
  • When a President is to be impeached for violation of the Constitution, the charge shall be preferred by either House of Parliament
  • Such charge must be contained in a resolution moved after 14 days notice, signed by at least 1/4 th of total members of the house, having a majority of atleast 2/3 rd
  • Nominated members of the house moving the resolution Can participate in impeachment, but they cannot participate in election of the president
  • Elected members of the Legislative assemblies of Delhi and Puducherry do not participate in impeachment, even though they participate in electing the president

Stand-in for President

Article 65
  • Vice-President shall act as President until the date on which a new President elected
  • When President is unable to discharge his duties due to absense. illness etc. Vice-President shall discharge his functions until the date on which the President resumes his duties
  • In the above cases Vice-President will enjoy all the priviledges of the President

Absence of Vice-President
  • In case offices of President and Vice-President are vacant at the same time The Chief Justice of India or in his absence, the Seniormost judge of the supreme court will discharges the function of President untill the vacany is filled
  • Justice M Hidayat-ul-lah discharged the functions of President from 20th July-24th August 1969, he is the only CJI to have ever done so

Election to fill vacancy

Article 62
  • Election to fill a vacancy caused by the expiration of the term of office shall be completed before the expiration of the term
  • Election to fill a vacancy in the office of President occurring by reason of his death, resignation or removal, or otherwise shall be held as soon as possible
  • and in no case later than six months from, the date of occurrence of the vacancy
  • The person elected to fill the vacancy will according to Article 56, be entitled to hold office for the full tenure

Powers and Functions

Executive Powers
Article 74
  • The Council of Ministers headed by the Prime Minister shall advise the President, who shall act in accordance to the said advice
Article 75
  • Article 75(I) Appoints the Prime Minister and other ministers on the advice of the Prime Minister
  • Article 75(IV) He administers the oath of the ministers
Article 76
  • Article 76(I) Appoints the Attorney General of India
Article 77
  • Article 77 (I)All executive actions of the Government of India are formally taken in his name
  • Article 77 (II)He can make rules specifying the manner in which the orders and other instruments made and executed in his name shall be authenticated
  • Article 77 (III)He can make rules for more convenient transaction of business of the Union Government, and for the allocation of said business among the ministers
Article 78
  • The Prime Minister shall communicate to the President all decisions of the Council of Ministers regarding administration of the Union
  • The President can call upon the Prime Minister to furnish such information
  • He can require the Prime minster to submit for the consideration of the Council of Ministers any matter on which a decision has been taken by a minister but not considered by the Council.
Power to Appoint
  • Appoints the Comptroller and Auditor General of India
  • Appoints the Cheif Justice of India
  • Appoints the Cheif Election Commissioner
  • Appoints the Chaiman of UPSC
  • Appoints Chairman of Finance Commission
  • Appoints Governor of the States
  • Can appoint Inter-State Council to promote Inter-State and Center-State cooperation
Powers Relating to SC and STs
  • He can create a commission to investigate into the condition of SC, ST and OBCs
  • Can declare any area as a Scheduled Area
  • Has power with respect to the administration of SC and ST areas

Legislative Powers
Article 85
  • Article 85(I) Summons both house of the parliament
  • Article 85(II) He can Prorouge either house of the Parliament. He can also Dissolve Lok Sabha
  • He can summon Joint Sitting of both houses of the parliament presided over by the Speaker of the Lok Sabha
  • Inaugural adress to the Parliment after every general election
  • Also addresses the Parliment at the first session of each year
  • He decides on questions of Disqualifying a member from the Parliament, after consulting with the election commission
  • These powers are formal, and by convention President uses these powers according to the advice of the Council of Ministers headed by the Prime Minister
  • In event of Hung parliament, the president invitesparties to command the required support anf form government. If still there is no majority he/she can call for a new election
Article 111
  • He can give assent to the Bill
  • He can withhold his assent to the Bill
  • He can return a bill to the parliament for reconsideration if it is Not a Money Bill
  • If the bill is passed after reconsideration, the President has to assent to it
Article 123
  • Can Promulgate ordinances when both the houses of parliament are not in session
  • These ordinances are interim and temporary in nature, their continuance is subject to approval of the parliament
  • They remain valid for Not more than 6 weeks from the date the parliament is convened
Power of Nominating
  • Can nominate two members to the Lok-Sabha from the Anglo Indian Community
  • Nominates 12 members to the Rajya Sabha from distinguished individuals
Permission for introducing Bills
  • His permission is required to introducing a Bill to alter boundaries of a State
  • Bill to Change the name of a State
  • Bill to Create a new State
  • Bill involving expenditure from the Consolidated Fund of India
Reports Laid Before parliament
  • Report of Comptroller and Auditor general of India
  • Report of the UPSC
  • Report of the National Commission for Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes
  • Report of Commissionor for Linguistic Minorities

Financial Powers
Article 112
  • He Causes the Annual Financial Statement (Union Budget) to be laid out before the parliament
Others
  • Money bills can be introduced in the parliament only with his/her prior recommendation
  • No demand for Grant can be made without his prior recommendation
  • He can make advances out of the Contingency Fund of India to meet any unforeseen expenditure
  • He constitutes a Finance Commission every five years to recommend the distribution of revenues between center and the states

Judicial Powers
Article 72
Pardon
Commutation
Remmission
Respite
Reprieve
Article 143
  • He can seek advice of the supreme court on any question of law or fact

Emergency Powers
National Emergency
  • By Article 352, can be declared on grounds of war, external aggression or armed rebellion
President Rule
  • By Article 356, 365, can be proclaimed on grounds of failure of constitutional machinery in the state
Financial Emergency
  • By Article 360, can be proclaimed if he is convinced that the financial stability or credit of India has been threatened
