Constitutional Development

The Company Rule (1773-1858)

Regulating Act of 1773
  • First step taken by the British Governmant to control and regulate affairs of the EIC in India
  • Designated the Governor of Bengal as the Governor-General of Bengal. First Governor-General was Lord Warren Hastings
  • It Subordinated the Governors of Bombay and Madras to the Governor-General of Bengal
  • Supreme Court was established in 1774 in Fort William Calcutta, Comprising of one chief justice and three other judges, Elijah Impey was the first chief justice
  • Prohibited a company servant from engaging in private trade, and taking bribes from the natives
  • Court of Directors (Govering body of the EIC) had to report on its revenue, civil and military affairs

Pitt's India Act of 1784
  • Introduced to remove the drawbacks of the Regulating act, named after British prime minister William Pitt
  • Distinguished between Commercial and political functions of the company
  • A new Board of Control was set up to manage the political affairs, while the Court of Directors managed the commercial affairs
  • Thus it established a system of Double Government
  • Empowered the Board of Control to supervise and direct all operations of civil and military government
  • For the first time the Company's territory in India were called British Possessions in India and the British Government had supreme control over Company's affairs nad its administration in India

Charter Act of 1813
  • Renewed the Company's Charter for another 20 years
  • Deprived the company of its monopoly trade with India
  • Subjected the three council of Governors to greater control of parliament
  • Christian Missionaries were allowed to spread their religion in India

Charter Act of 1833
  • It made the Governeor General Of Bengal as the Governor General Of India and his council as the India council. First Governor General Of India was Lord William Bentick
  • Deprived the governors of Bombay and Madras of legislative powers
  • EIC was reduced to an administrative and political entity
  • This Act for the First time creatd a Government of India with the Governor General on the head having authority over entire territorial area possessed by the British in India

Charter Act of 1853
  • It seperated the legislative and executive functions of the Governor-General Council
  • New legislative council of Governor-General comprising of 6 new members called Legislative Councillors, this came to be known as the Indian Legislative Council
  • Local representation in the Indian Legislative Council, 4 of the 6 members were to be appointed by the provincial governments of Madras, Bombay, Bengal and Agra.
  • Introduced Open competition system for recruitment of civil servants, and was open to Indians also. Accordingly the Macaulay Committee (1854) was appointed.
  • British Parliament was empowered to put company's governance at an end at any time

The Crown Rule (1858-1947)

Government Of India Act of 1858
  • This act was enacted in the wake of Sepoy Mutiny and Replaced rule of EIC by rule of the British Crown
  • Ended the system of double government, by Abolishing the Board of Control and Court of Directors
  • Changed designation of Governor-General to Viceroy, who was the direct representative of the British crown in India, Lord Canning was the first viceroy
  • Created a new office for the Secretary of State for India, vested with complete authority and control of the administration. He would be a member of the British cabinet
  • Established a 15 member Council of India, whose chairman was the Secretary of State for India

Indian Councils Act of 1861
  • It made a Begining of Representative Institutions in India
  • It provided that the Viceroy's extended legislative council should have some Indians as non offical members
  • The then Viceroy Lord Canning nominated Raja of Benaras, Maharaja Of Patiala and Sir Dinkar Rao
  • Initiated the process of decentralization by restoring legislative powers to the Bombay and Madras precidencies
  • Provided for establishment of new legislative councils for Bengal, North Western region and Punjab

Indian Councils Act of 1892
  • Increased the number of non-official members in the central and provincial legislative councils, but maintained official majority in them
  • Made a limited but indirect provision for "elected" (nominated) (recommended by municipalities, merchant bodies and universities) non-official members
  • Those "elected" members had no right to vote or raise questions in councils

Indian Councils Act of 1909
  • Called as Morley-Minto Reforms
  • The number of members of the central legislative council was increased to 60 from 16
  • Right of seperate electorate given to Muslims, thus it legalized communalism, Lord Minto came to be known as Father of Communal Electorate
  • Official members were to retain majority in central, but in provinces non-official members would be the majority
  • For the First time an Indian was admitted into the Executive council of the Viceroy. Satyendra Prasad Sinha became the first Indian to join the Viceroy's executive council

Government Of India Act of 1919
  • Called as Montague-Chelmsford Reforms
  • Demarcated and seperated Central and Provintial subjects
  • Furthur divided provintial subjects into Transferred subjects and Reserved subjects, this was called as Dyarchy
  • Introduced Bicameralism in Indian Legislative Council, it was divided into Upper House (Council Of State) and Lower House (Legislative Assembly)
  • Three of Six members of the Viceroy's Executive Council were to be Indian
  • Extended seperate electorate for Sikhs, Indian Christians, Anglo-Indians and Europeans
  • Provided for High Commissioner for India, representing India in Great Britain
  • Seperated Provintial and Central budgets
  • Central Public Service Commission was to be set up in 1926
  • Appointed statutory commission to look into and report on the working of this act after 10 years

Simon Commission 1927
  • Seven member statutory commission headed by John Simon
  • Boycotted by all parties as all seven members were British
  • It proposed the establishment of Federation of British India and Princely States
  • Abolition of Dyarchy
  • Continuation of communal electorate

Nehru Report of 1928
  • Memorandum outlining proposed dominion status Constitution for India
  • Prepared by the All party conference chaired by Motilal Nehru (Feb 1928)
  • Contained Bill of rights
  • No state religion, men women to have equal rights as citizens
  • Federal form of government with reidual power vested in center
  • No seperate electorate, for any community or minorities
  • Suggested that provinces should be linguistically determined

Communal Award
  • Extension of the idea of communal electorate to the depressed classes
  • Announced by Ramsay Macdonald, British Prime Minister in 1932
  • Leaders of Congerss and depressed classes made an agreement known as Poona Pact
  • Hindu joint electorate was restored, and seats were reserved for the depressed classes

Government Of India Act of 1935
  • Provided for the establishment of All India Federation, it divided powers between centre and units in terms of Federal List, Provintial List and Concurrent List, The AIF did not come into being as the Princely States did not join it
  • Abolished Dyarchy in provinces, and intoduced Provintial autonomy, came into effect in 1937, discontinued in 1939
  • Introduced Bicameralism in six out of eleven provinces, thus legislature of Bengal, Bombay, Madras, Bihar, Assam, and United provinces were divided into Upper and Lower house
  • Further extended the idea of Communal electorate, by providing seperate electorate for Depressed classes, women and labours
  • Abolished the Council of India, set up by 1858 GOI act, the Secretary of State was provided with a team of advisors.
  • Extended franchise, about 10% of the population got voting rights
  • Provided for the establishment of Reserve Bank of India
  • Provided for the establisment of Provintial and Joint Public Service Commission
  • Provided for the establishment of Federal Court

August Offer of 1940
  • In August 1940 Viceroy Lord Linlithgow made the offer
  • After WWII a representative Indian body would be set up for framing the constitution of India
  • Viceroy's executive council would be expanded without delay

Cripps Mission of 1942
  • Draft declaration of the proposals of the British Government after end of WWII
  • March 1942 Sir Stafford Cripps came with the draft
  • The Constitution of India was to be framed by an elected Constituent Assembly

The Cabinet Mission of 1946
  • British Prime Minister Lord Clement Atlee sent a cabinet mission, consisting of three cabinet members
  • Lawrence, Cripps and Alexander
  • Instructed on the procedure to be followed by the Constitution drafting body
  • Rejected the idea of a seperate Constituent Assembly and seperate electorate for Muslims

Mountbatten Plan of 1947
  • Announced by the British Government on 3rd June 1947
  • Principle of Partition of India was accepted by the British Government
  • Successor Governments would be given dominion status
  • Implicit right to secede from the British Commonwealth
  • The Indian Independence Act was the implementation of the 3rd June plan

Indian Independence Act of 1947
  • Came into force on 18th July 1947
  • Divided British Indian territory into India and Pakistan, which were to be dominions under the British Commonwealth unitl their constitutions came into effect
  • To demarcate the boundary a Commission under the chairmanship of Sir Cyril Radcliffe was formed
  • Abolished the office of the Viceroy, and provided for eash dominion a Governor-general
  • Lord Mountbatten was the first Governor-general of dominion India
  • C Rajagopalachari was the first Governor-general of independent India

The Constituent Assembly

Framing the Constitution
  • Idea for constituent assembly was first put forward by MN Roy
  • Demand for Constituent assembly was first met by the British in August Offer
  • Cripps Mission accepted that the constituent assembly to be made up of Indians only
  • Constituent Assembly set up as per the Cabinet Mission plan on November 1946
  • Total 389 members, 296 were elected and the rest nominated by the Princely States
  • First Meeting was held on 11th December 1946, with Sachidanand Sinha as the president
  • Objective resolution moved by Jawaharlal Nehru
  • Drafting Committee appointed on 29th August 1947, with Dr B.R Ambedkar as the president
  • It took almost three years for the drafting of the constitution of India
  • On November 26 1949 the people through the Constituent Assembly Adopted, enacted and gave themselvs the constitution
  • Constituent Assembly adopted National Anthem, and National Song on 24th January 1950
  • Constituent Assembly, on 24th January 1950, elected Dr Rajendra Prasad as the First President of free India
  • On 24th January 1950 the constitution was signed by all the members of the Constituent Assembly
  • The Constitution Came into full Operation on the 26th January 1950
