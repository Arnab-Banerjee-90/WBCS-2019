Indus Valley Civilization (2500-1700 B.C)

Nomenclature and Time period
  • Harappan Civilization Named by John Marshall
  • Bronze Age Calcholithic civilization
  • 2500 - 1700 B.C
  • The ruins of Harappa were first described in 1842 by Charles Masson
  • In 1872â€“75, Alexander Cunningham published the first Harappan seal
  • Alexander Cunningham called the "Father of Indian Archeology"
  • He was the founder of Archeological Society of India
  • According to John Marshall Indus was the oldest civilization
  • According to Will Durant Indus civilization was older than that of the Nile

Origins
  • Mehergarh in Baluchistan (Oldest Neolithic settlement in Indian subcontinent 7000 B.C)

Geographical Spread
  • West:-Sutkagendor (Pak-Iran border)
  • East:-Alamgirpur (Uttarpradesh)
  • North:-Manda (Jammu Kashmir)
  • South:-Daimabad (Maharashtra)
  • Mundigak and Shortughai are two sites located in Afganisthan

Characteristics of Cities

Town Planning
  • Non-uniform
  • Grid system based
  • Divided into two parts Citadel and Lower part
  • Citadel was on the western part, housing public buildings and members of the ruling class
  • On the Eastern side lay the Lower part inhabited by the common people
  • Houses two-storied or more generally had side entrances
  • No windows faced the main street
  • Large-scale use of burnt bricks
  • In Lothal, Kalibangan no tiny cage like living quarters have been discovered, which infers that these two cities were the most liberal

Drainage System
  • Impressive drainage system
  • Water flowed from houses to the streets, which had drains
  • Almost all houses had own courtyard and bathroom
  • Underground drainage system connected all houses to street drains
  • Drains were built of burnt bricks

Script
  • Pictographic
  • Fish symbol mostly represented
  • Alternate Right to left then left to right: Boustrophedon

Agriculture
  • Wheat, barley,dates,peas, millet, ragi, bajra, jowar etc
  • Rice husks found in Lothal and Rangpur (Gujarat)
  • Sugarcane not known to the Indus people
  • First to produce cotton called as Sindon by the Greeks

Religious Life
  • Mother Godess chief female diety
  • Pashupati Mahadeva (Proto-Shiva) chief male diety
  • In the seal depicting Pashumati-Shiva, he is surrounded by Tiger, elephant, Rhino, Buffalo and Deer
  • No temples or other religious structures have been found except the great bath
  • Prevalance of Lingam and Yoni worship
  • Trees(pipal), animals(bulls), birds(doves,piegons) were worshipped
  • Did not worship the forces of nature

Trade And Animal Rearing
  • Barter system
  • Linear system of measurement was used
  • Weights and measures were in multiples of sixteen
  • Mentioned as Meluha in Sumerian literature
  • Domesticated buffaloes, oxens, asses, sheep, goat, pig ,elephant
  • Did not know about usage of Horse
  • They did not know about Lion

Burial Practices
  • Harappa :- East West axis; R-37 and H cemetary
  • Mahenjodaro :- Three forms of burial: Complete, Fractional and post cremation
  • Kalibangan :- Circular and rectangular grave
  • Lothal :- Double burial

Art and Craft
  • Bead making
  • Pottery both plain (red) and painted (red and black)
  • Seals, mostly steatite imagesof humpless bulls,buffalo,tiger
  • Terracota Figurines
