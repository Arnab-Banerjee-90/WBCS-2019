Great Revolt of 1857

Early Precedents
  • In 1806 sepoys of Vellore mutinied
  • In 1824 the 47th Regiment of Sepoys at Barrackpore refused to go to Burma by sea
  • In 1844 seven battalions revolted on the questions of Bhata and salaries
  • There were mutinies in 1825 (Assam), 1838 (Sholapur), 1844 (Sind) and 1849-59 (Punjab) as well

Causes of the Revolt

Social and Religious cause
  • The use of cartriges greased with beef or pork fat
  • Interference with socio-religious life of Indians, like Abolition of Sati (1829), Widow remarriage act (1856)
  • Religious Disabilities Act of 1850 which modified the Hindu law and allowed Christian missionariesto enter India

Economic Causes
  • Breakdown of the village economy
  • Commercialization of agriculture
  • Drainage of Wealth

Military Grievances
  • General Service Enlistment act of 1856 made it compulsory for the sepoys to cross the seas
  • Post Office act of 1854, withdrew free postal facilities for them
  • They were required to to serve infar away places without extra bhata

Political Cause
  • Doctrine of Lapse introduced by Lord Dalhousie
  • Adopted sons of the deceased kings were not recognised as heirs to the throne
  • Dalhousie annexed Awadh in 1856 on grounds of misrule
  • 3/5 th Of the Bengal army belonged from Awadh
  • He abolished the titles of the Nawab of Carnatic and the Raja of Travancore
  • Refused to pay pension to Dhondu Pant (Nanasahib), the adopted son of last Peshwa Baji Rao II
