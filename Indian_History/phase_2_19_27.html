Struggle for Independence (1919-1927)

Rowlatt Act, 1919
  • Chelmsford appointed committee under chairmanship of Justice Sydney Rowlatt in 1917
  • Investigate the nature of revolutionary activities and measures to curb the same
  • This committee was known as the Sedition or Rowlatt Committee
  • As per the act it gave authority to the government
    • To arrest a person without warrant
    • Search any place
    • There could be no appeal against the decision of court
    • Detention without trial for a maximum period of two years

Anti-Rowlatt Satyagraha
  • Gandhiji launched campaign against it by forming Satyagraha sabha on 24th February 1919 in Bombay
  • Gandhiji inaugurated his satyagraha by obseving a day of hartal
  • This move was criticised by the moderate leaders of the INC like SN Banerjee, DE Wacha, Anie Besant etc.
  • Liberals though it would hamper the reforms of the British government
  • Date of Hartal was fixed to 6th April 1919, In Delhi it was observed on 30th March
  • In Amritsar DR Kitchlew, Dr Satyapal were arrested
  • This led to mob violence in Punjab, government buildings were set on fire, 5 Englishmen were murdered

Jalianwalabagh Massacre
  • After the hartal of 6th April, Punjab was facing a violent situation
  • Civil government handed over the administration to the military authorities under General Dyer
  • Dyer banned all public meetings and detained all important political leaders
  • On 13th April 1919, Dyer ordered to open fire on unarmed civilians gathered to peacefully protest the arrest of DR Kitchlew, Dr Satyapal
  • 379 people were dead according to official figures
  • Rabindranath Tagore surrendered Knighthood conferred upon him by the British government
  • Sir Sankaran Nair, former president of the INC resigned form his membership of the Viceroy's executive council
  • Gandhiji withdrew the Satyagraha and said 'It was my Himalayan blunder'
  • Hunter Committee was appointed to look into this massacre, Gandhiji called the Hunter committee report as 'Official Whitewash'

Khilafat Movement, 1919
  • Muslim sentiments were hurt after Turkish defeat in WWI
  • Indian Muslim sentiments flared up after there was revolts against the Turkish sultans instigated by the British
  • Main demand of the Khilafatists was that the Ottoman caliph should retain his empire with sufficient temporal power to defend Islam

Course of the movement
  • In April-May 1919 All India Khilafat conference was set up in Bombay
  • At a conference in Luckonw All India Khilafat Committee was set up as Seth Chhotani as the president and Maulana Shaukat Ali as the Secretary
  • Gandhiji was elected President of the All India Khilafat Conference held in November 1919
  • Amritsar session of the INC in 1919 gave great boost to the movement
  • Gandhi succeeded in persuading the INC to support the movement
  • At the special Calcutta session of INC in 1920 Congrss endorsed the programme of Non-cooperation
  • At the Nagpur Session of the INC in December 1920 the calcutta endorsement was ratified
  • INC outlined 4 stages of Non-Cooperation
    • Resignation of titles and honorary posts
    • Resignation from civil services under the government
    • Resignation from Police and Army services
    • Non payment of taxes for redressal of the Punjab grievances
  • All India Khilafat conference held at Karachi on 8th July 1921 called upon Muslim soldiers in the Indian Army to quit their jobs

Non-Cooperation Movement
  • Congress accepted to launch Non-Cooperation movement for the two Wrongs
  • Punjab Issue and the Khilafat issue

Opposition to NCM
  • Lala Lajpat Rai opposed the programme of educational boycott
  • CR Das opposed the boycott of legislature
  • MM Malavia, Jinnah opposed the goal of Swaraj

Significance of the Nagpur session
  • The session started from 26th December 1920
  • Two important ammendments to the Constitution of Congress was made
    1. Goal of Congress was changed to Attainment of Swaraj by peaceful and legitimate means
    2. Revolutionary changes were brought about in the Congress organisation
      • Working committee of 15 members
      • Formation of All India Congress Committee of 300 members
      • Creation of a hierarchy of district, talukas and village Congress Committee
      • Opening Congress membership to all adults, men and women above 21 years on payment of annual subscription of 4 annas
  • AICC meeting at Vijayawada on 31st March 1921 outlined two more programmes
    1. Collection of 1Crore rupees for the Tilak Memorial Swarajya fund
    2. To introduce 20lakh charkhas into the Indian household

Growth and Spread of the Movement
  • First real mass movement launched under Mahatma Gandhi
  • Many leading lawyers including CR Das, Motilal Nehru, Vallabhai Patel gave up their practices
  • Between January and March 1921, the districts of Rae Bereli, Pratapgarh, Faizabad and Sultanpur witnessed widespread Agrarian riots under the leadership of Baba Ram Chandra
  • In late 1921 there was another agrarian movement known as Eka Movement under Madari Pasi
  • Bijolia Movement and Bhil Movement under Motilal Tejavat gathers impetus from Non-cooperation movement
  • Programme to start civil disbobedience was approved by INC at Allahabad session
  • Bardoli Taluq was the chosed place from where Gandhiji would launch the civil disobedience campaign
  • On 5th February, Congress volunteers were fired at by the police at Chauri Chaura at Gorakhpur in Uttarpradesh
  • In retaliation, the infuriated mob killed 12 policemen
  • Congress working committee met at Bardoli on 12th February 1922, and called off the movement, popularly known as Bardoli Resolution
  • Subhash Chandra Bose called it A National Calamity

Swarajya Party
  • In December 1922 the Gaya Session of the Congess was held
  • It became a battle-ground between the No-changers (C Rajagopalachari) and the Pro-Changers
  • The Pro-Changers were defeated
  • CR Das, Motilal Nehru resigned
  • Das and Nehru created Swarajya party on 31st December 1922
  • CR Das became its first president and Motilal Nehru its secretary
  • In the Legislative assembly since the Swarajists were not in majority, a coalition of seventy members was formed, known as Nationalist party they were supported by the independents and by Jinnah

Objective of the Swarajists
  • Immediate objective was speedy attainment of full dominion status

Achievements of Swarajists
  • In the very first Session Nehru put forward the national demand for framing a new constitution
  • Rangachari moved a resolution requesting the Governor-General in council to revise the Act of 1919 to secure for India provincial autonomy and full dominiom status
  • Swarajists forced the appointment of Sir Alexander Muddiman committee to innquire into the defects of the act of 1919 and to suggest remedies
  • Succeeded in throwing out the budget, forcing the government to rely on the party's power of certification
  • In march 1925 the Swarajista managed to elect Vithalbhai Patel a leading Swarajist as the President of hthe Central Legislatve Assembly
  • Lala Lajpat Rai joined the Central legislative assembly as Swarajist and accepted the role of Deputy Leadership of the party
  • ML Nehru accepted the membership of the Skeen Committee

Resignation
  • The Swarajists finally walked out of the legislature in 1930

Muddiman Committee (1924)
  • Finance bill was rejected in 1924
  • Swarajists led by Motilal Nehru introduced introduced an ammendment in 1924
  • They demanded the framing of Indian Constitution by a Constituent Assembly
  • As a response Government appointed 9 member commission led by Air Alexander Muddiman to reform the working of Dyarchy of the Montague-Chelmsford reforms

Butler Commission (1927)
  • In 1927 the perople of the princely states formed the State Peoples Conference
  • It was done with a view to introducing self-government institution
  • Princes sought help of the government
  • The result was setting up of three member committee led by Harcourt Butler
  • The committee laid stress on preservation of Princely statesthrough British Paramountacy
