Anglo-French Conflict

First Carnatic War
  • It was under the influence of Austrian war of succession
  • Treaty of Aix-la-Chapelle (1748) brought the Austrian war of succession to an end
  • English got back Madras and French got Louisberg region of North America

Second Carnatic War
  • Conflict began with the issue of disputed succession to the throne of Carnatic
  • French supported Chanda Sahib and British supported Mohammed Ali
  • In the following war, the French were defeated and Chanda Sahib was beheaded
  • In 1754 Godeau replaced Dupleix as the French Governor

Third Carnatic War
  • Final phase started in 1758 when 7 years war broke out in Europe
  • Count-de-Lally the French Governor general captured Fort St. David in 1758
  • The English under General Eyre Coote decisively defeated the French in the battle of Wandiwash on 22nd Jan 1760
  • English captured Pondicherry, the French capital, and French settlement of Mahe and Jinji
  • Treaty of Paris was signed in 1763, according to which Pondicherry and some other French settlements were returned

Anglo-Mysore Conflict
  • Earlier part of Vijaynagara Empire
  • After 1565, Wodeyar dynasty found its rule
  • Usurpation conspiracy started under rulership of Chakka Krishnaraja by Devaraja and Nanjaraja

First Anglo-Mysore War (1767-69)
  • Rise of Haider Ali was a source for British concern
  • Haider's ambition was to drive off the British from the Carnatic
  • British formed the Tripartite alliance with the Marathas and the Nizam against Haider
  • Haider bought the Marathas and allured the Nizam against the British
  • Haider was victorious in the war and appeared near the gates of Madras
  • The British signed the Treaty of Madras in 1769 with Haider
    • Raja of Tanjore was to to considered an ally of haider
    • Captured employees of the Madras Government were to be released
    • Restitution of all stated except Karur

Second Anglo-Mysore War (1780-84)
  • Mutual distrust between Haider and British
  • Refusal of English to fulfill the terms of the treaty when Haider was attacked by Marathas in 1771
  • Haider found the French more helpful in providing for meeting his military demands for gun, saltpetre and lead
  • Haider established a close alliance with the French
  • Warren Hastings was suspicious about Haider's relation with the French
  • English attempted to capture Mahe, which was under Haider's protection
  • Haider formed a front with the Nizam and Marathas in 1780, attacked Carnatic and captured Arcot
  • Defeated English army under Bailie
  • Englsih detached the Nizam and Marathas from Haider in 1781
  • Hyder was defeated by Sir Eyre Coote in the battle of Porto Novo
  • In the following year Haider defeated English army under Braithwaite, Haider died while the war was in progress
  • Tipu Sultan continued the war
  • It finally led to Treaty of Mangalore in 1784

Third Anglo-Mysore War (1790-92)
  • Growing power of Mysore and Tipu made the British apprehensive
  • Lord Cornwallis worked on anti-tipu suspicion and arranged a triple alliance againt Tipu with Marathas and Nizam
  • Tipu sought the help of the Turks by sending an embassy to Constantinopole in 1784 and to the French King in 1787
  • His differnence with Travancore grew after the King of Travancore violated his soverign rights
  • Tipu attacked Travancore in 1790, and seeing an oppertunity the English sided with Travncore and declared war against Tipu
  • Tipu defeated general Meadows in 1790
  • Cornwallis himself took command of the English army and headed towards Srirangapatnam
  • Tipu found it impossible to prolong the struggle and signed the Treaty of Srirangapatnam in 1792
    • Tipu lost half of Mysorean territory
    • He was to pay indemnity of ₹ 3 crore
    • He was to release all prisoners of war

Fourth Anglo-Mysore War (1799)
  • To enlist the support of France Tipu enlisted in the Jacobin club
  • Planted tree of liberty at Srirangapatna
  • Sent embassies to Arabia, Zaman Shah of Afganisthan, Constantinopole, Directory of Versailles and the French in the isle of French seized Mauritus
  • Lord Wellesly the new Governor general was determined to get rid of him
  • War against Tipu began in 1799, and he was defeated by Stuart Sedaseer and General Harris at Malvelley
  • He died defending his capital of Srirangapatnam on the 4th of May 1799

Occupation of Bengal

Siraj Vs. British
  • The company used Dastaks, issued by the Mughal dynasty in 1717 illegaly, as an excuse to avoid taxes to the Bengal nawab
  • The British interfered with the Nawab's court and supported one of the opposers of the nawab Ghaseti Begum (Siraj's aunt)
  • British policy to help merchants like Jagath Seth to consolidate their own base
  • Mir Jafar pretended loyalty to Alivardi Khan's successor Siraj Ud Daulah, but betrayed him to the British in the battle of Plassey.

Mir Jafar Ali Khan
  • First Najafi Nawab and successor to Siraj
  • Remembered as Gaddar-e-Hind
  • Jafar failed to satisfy constant British demands for money
  • He sought Dutch aid to get rid of the British
  • In October 1760, the company forced him to abdicate in favor of Qasim
  • However, Qasim's independent spirit and plan to force the East India company out of his dominion led to his overthrow
  • Jafar was restored as the Nawab in 1763
  • Jafar ruled till his death in 1765
  • Najm-ud-Daulah was the Nawab of Bengal, Bihar and Orissa from 1765 to 1766. He was the second son of Mir Jafar

Mir Qasim
  • Replaced Mir Jafar, his father-in-law as the Nawab of Bengal in 1760
  • Mir Qasim abolished taxes on the local traders
  • He transferred his capital from Murshidabad to Munger
  • Sought to modernise his army in the European pattern

Battle Of Buxar
  • Mir Qasim allied with Shuja-ud-daula and Shah Alam II against the British
  • Fought on 22 October 1764
  • British army under Hector Munro defeated the alliance
  • Shuja-ud-daula and Shah Alam II surrendered and the war ended with the Treaty of Allahabad in 1765

Treaty of Allahabad
  • Signed on 12th August 1765 between Shah Alam II and Robert Clive
  • Alam granted the East India Company Diwani rights, or the right to collect taxes on behalf of the Emperor from the eastern province of Bengal-Bihar-Orissa
  • In return, the Company agrred to pay an annual tribute of twenty-six lakh rupees to Shah Alam II
  • The Nawab of Awadh also had to pay fifty-three lakhs of rupees as war indemnity to the East India Company.

Anglo-Maratha Conflict

First Anglo-Maratha War
  • Began with the Treaty of Surat in 1775
  • Ended with Treaty of Salbai in 1782

Second Anglo-Maratha War
  • Lord Wellesly imposed Subsidiary alliance on the peshwa
  • Other Maratha chief finding this alliance humiliating, declared war on the British
  • Treaty of Bassein was signed on 31st December 1802 betwwen the Peshwa Baji Rao II and the East India Company
  • Treaty of Deogaon signed on 17th December 1803 between Raghuji Bhonsle II and East India Company
  • The British won the Battle of Assaye under Arthur Wellesley
  • The Treaty of Surji-Anjangaon was signed on December 30, 1803 between the British and Daulat Rao Sindhia
  • In 1804 Yashwant Rao Holkar attempted to fight against the British with a coalition of Indian rulers, but it failed
  • He was the last to sign a treaty with the British, on 24 December 1805 at a place called Rajpur Ghat on the bank of Beas River. The treaty was named 'The Treaty of Peace and Amity between the British Government and Yashwantrao Holkar.'

Third Anglo-Maratha War
  • Lord Hastings reassumed the aggressive policies of Lord Wellesly to proclaim British paramountacy in the Maratha region
  • Maratha Confedaracy was dissolved, the Peshwa Bajirao II was dethroned and pensioned off
  • Treaty of Peace between the Honorable the East India Company and His Highness Maharajah Mulhar Rao Holkar was signed, known as Treaty of Mandsaur in 1818

Anglo-Sikh Conflict

Ranjit Singh
  • Rose to power in 1792
  • Organised an alliance of Sikh Misis west of the Sutlej in 1798
  • Repelled the attack of Afgan ruler Zaman Shah
  • Accepted as maharaja of Punjab in 1801
  • Death of Ranjit Singh was followed by weak rulers and political instability

First Anglo-Sikh War
  • English invaded Punjab and occupied Lahore
  • Dictated a peace treaty known as Lahore Treaty in 1846
  • Sikhs were to renounce all their territories south of the Sutlej
  • War indemnity of 1.5 crore was imposed on Lahore Durbar
  • Unable to pay the amount, the region between Beas and Indus was given to the British
  • Gulab Singh was instrumental in the negotiations, thus the Company gave him Kashmir

Second Anglo-Sikh War
  • Under Dalhousie the British forces attacked Punjab in the pretext of Mulraj Rebellion in 1848
  • Punjab was annexed in 1849
  • Dalip Singh the minor son of Ranjit Singh was pensioned off and sent to London

Anglo-Nepal Relations
  • Frequent raids by the Gorkhas in the British territories
  • In May 1814 Lord Hastings declared war on the Gorkhas
  • General Gardener won over Almora, while General Ochterlony defeated Gorkha leader Amar Singh Thapa
  • Treaty of Saguli was signed in 1815 but the Gorkhas did not ratify it
  • This led to second expedition against Gorkhas in 1816
  • General Ochterlony defeated the Gorkhas in the Battle of Makwanpur
  • Gorkhas now accepted the treaty of Saguli
  • The English acquired the hill stations of Nainital, Almora, Ranikhet etc

Anglo-Afgan Conflict

First Anglo-Afgan war
  • Auckland signed tripartite treaty with Shah Shuja and Ranjit Singh in 1838
  • Afagnis did not acccept Shah Shuja as chief
  • Afganis rebelled and killed Englishmen Burns, Charles, Sir William
  • British forced to sign humiliating treaty in 1842 to recognise Dost Mohammed as Amir of Afganisthan

Second Anglo-Afgan war
  • Sher Ali the chief, rejected English missions sent by Lord Lytton but accepted Russian missions instead
  • British attacked from three sides, khyber pass, Bolan pass and Kurram pass. Sher Ali was defeated and died later on
  • Yakub Khan signed the treaty of Gandamak in 1879
  • British occupied Kabul and Abdur Rehman became the new Amir of Afganisthan

Third Anglo-Afgan war
  • In 1919 Habibullah the chief, was murdered
  • His son Amanullah tried to declare his independence from British regency
  • He invaded British territories on German instigation, but was defeated
  • Treaty of Rawalpindi was signed accordiing to which Amirs were free to pursue their policy within their territory, but their annual subsidies was stopped
