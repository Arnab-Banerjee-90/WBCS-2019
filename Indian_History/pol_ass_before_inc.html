Political Associations Before Indian National Congress

Before 1857

Bangabhasha Prakashika Sabha
  • Established in 1836
  • Founded by associated of Rammohan Roy
  • Dwarkanath Tagore was one of its most active members
  • Said to be the first organised political association of India
  • Worked in co-operation with the British India Society in London

Zamindari Association
  • Founded in Calcutta in 1837
  • To safeguard the interests of the landlords through constitutional agitation

British India Society
  • Founded in England by efforts of William Adams in 1839
  • In 1841 it started publishing a newspaper British Indian Advocate

British Indian Association
  • Founded by George Thompson in 1851
  • It was the merging of Zamindari Association and Bengal British India Society
  • It was the most important political body of India during the 1857 revolt
  • Its Organ the Hindu Patriot was the most influential newspaper

Bombay Association
  • Founded by Jaganath Shunkerseth in 1852
  • First political organization of the Bombay Presidency

Madras Native Association
  • Founded by GL Chetty in 1852
  • Made demands similar to that of British India Association

After 1857

East India Association
  • Founded by Dadabhai Naoroji in 1866
  • Its aim was to populariza Indian grievances so as to influence British public opinion

India League
  • Founded by Sisir Kumar Ghosh, the founder and editor of Amrita Bazar Patrika in 1868

Poona Sarvajanik Sabha
  • Founded by MG Ranade and GV Joshi in 1870
  • In 1875 its members submitted a petition to the house of commons demanding India's direct representation in British Parliament

Indian Association
  • Founded by Surendranath Banerjee and Anandamohan Basu in 1876
  • Most important political association before the Indian National Congress
  • Center for all India movement of Agitation issues like lowering age limit of Civil Service Exam, and Ilbert Bill controversy
  • It seriously tried to become an all India body
  • Sponsored all India conference in 1883 and 1885 in Calcutta

Ilbert Bill
  • Criminal Procedure Ammendment Code of 1883
  • Aimed to give Indian Judges and magistrates to try European and British offenders in criminal cases
  • Strong protest against this bill by the European community
  • Lord Ripon had to modify the bill
  • European British subjects accused of Criminal offence were given right to trial bu a jury in which half the members were to be Europeans or Americans
  • This intensified the grwing feeling of nationalism and unity among the Indians
