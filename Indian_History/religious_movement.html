Religious Movement

Jainism

Origins
  • Founded by Rishavnath (1st Tirthankar)
  • There were a total of 24 Tirthankars
  • Rig Veda mentions only 2 Rishava and Aristhameni (22nd)
  • Parshwanath was the 23rd Tirthankar
    • Prince of Benaras who lived the life of a hermit
    • Died at Sammet-Shikar,Giridh,Jharkhand
    • Teachings were Ahimsa,Asteya,Satya,Aparigraha Known as Chaturyam
  • Mahavira was the 24th Tirthankar who adopted the above teachings and added Brahmacharya (Chastity)

Life of Mahavira (540 - 468 B.C)
  • Born to Siddharta and Trishla at Kundalgram near Vaishali
  • His real name was Bardhaman
  • Married to Yashoda and Priyadarsena was their daughter
  • Renounciation at age 30 became ascetic and joined order founded by 23rd Tirthankar Parshwanath
  • Attained Kaivalya at 42 under sal tree at Jimbikagram by the bank of Rijukalpa river
  • First sermon at Pava (near Rajagriha)
  • Was called Jaina or Jitendriya
  • He died in Pavapuri

Jain Philisophy

Three Ratnas/Jewels to attain Kaivalya
  • Samyak Viswas (Right Faith)
  • Samyak Gyan (Right Knowledge)
  • Samyak Karma (Right Action)

Five Cardinal Principles
  • Ahimsa (Non-Injury)
  • Satya (Truthfulness)
  • Asteya (Non-Stealing)
  • Aparigraha (Non-possession of property)
  • Brahmacharya (Celibacy/Non-Adultery)
  • Mahavratas are the monks and Anuvratas are the lay members

Sects Of Jainism
  • Bhadrabahu (in South)insisted retention of nudity his followers are Digambaras
  • Sthulbhadra (in North )renounced nudity his followers are Svetambaras

Buddhism

Life of Gautam Buddha
  • Born in 563 B.C at Lumbini
  • Father Suddhadhana and mother Mahamaya, wife Yashodhara and son Rahul
  • His real name was Siddharta
  • Renounced family life at 29
  • Nirvana at 35, at Uruvela under pipal tree (Bodh-Gaya)
  • First sermon (Dhramachakra Pravartana) at Sarnath
  • Death at 80, at Kusinagar

Buddhist Philosophy

Triratnas
  • Buddha (The Enlightened)
  • Dhamma (Doctrine)
  • Sangha (Order)

    • Four Noble Truths
  • The world is full of sorrow
  • Desire is the root cause of sorrow
  • Cessation of sorrow is possible
  • The path to cessation of sorrow is Ashtangik Marga

    • Ashtangik Marga
  • Right Views
  • Right Aspirations
  • Right Speech
  • Right Conduct
  • Right Effort
  • Right Livelihood
  • Right Mindfulness
  • Right Meditation

    • Sects Of Buddhism

    Hinayana or Lesser Wheel
  • Did not believe in idol-worship
  • Religion without God, Karma taking the place of God
  • Pali language was used by Hinayana Buddhists
  • Patronised by Ashoka

    • Mahayana or Greater Wheel
  • Believed in the heavenliness of Buddha and in idol-worship
  • Sanskrit language was used by Hinayana Buddhists
  • Patronised by Kanishka, Harsha

    • Vajrayana or Vehicle of Thunderbolt
  • Believed that salvation could be best attained by acquiring the magical power of Vajra
  • Chief divinities were the Taras
  • Became popular in East India

    • Buddhist Scriptures

    Sutta Pitaka
  • A collection of Buddha's Sermons
  • Divided into 5 Nikayas (Volumes)

    • Vinaya Pitaka (Discipline Basket)
  • Contains rules and regulations of monastic discipline for monks

    • Abhidharma Pitaka (Special Teachings)
  • Ascribed to Moggaliputta Tissa
  • Contains profound philosophy of Buddha's teachings
