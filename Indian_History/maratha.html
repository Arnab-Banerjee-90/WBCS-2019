The Maratha Empire

Life of Shivaji
  • Born in Shivner in 1627
  • Shahji Bhonsle was his father, who was a military officer in the State of Ahmednangar
  • Sivaji was brought up in Poona under supervision of Dadaji Konadev
  • Also came under religious influence of Guru Samarth Ramdas
  • Captured Toorna fort in 1646, and built new forts like Raigarh and Pratapgarh
  • Coronated at Raigarh in 1674, and assumend the title of Haindava-dharma-dharak

Conquests of Shivaji
  • In 1656 conquered Javli from Maratha chief Chandra Rao More
  • Attacked the Adil-Shah kingdom of Bijapore in 1657-58, killed Afzal Khan in his own camp
  • Shaista Khan was appointed Governor of Deccan to check the rising power of Shivaji
  • In 1663 Shivaji attacked military camp of Shaista Khan, and plundered Surat in 1664
  • Aurangzeb sent his Prince Muazzam against Shivaji, which was a failure
  • Mirja Raja Jai Singh was sent thereafter, and he succeeded in in surrounding Shivaji in the fort of Purandhar
  • Treaty of Purandhar was signed in 1665
  • Shivaji renewed his war in the form of second plunder of Surat in 1670, and gradually regained many of his lost forts
  • Aurangzeb sent Diler Khan and Bahadur Khan against Shivaji, however Shivaji defeated them in the Battle of Salher in 1672
  • He formed alliance with the Qutub Shahi rulers of Golconda and led a campaign into Bijapuri Karnataka, conquering Jingi and many other forts in Karnataka
  • Died in Raigarh in 1680

Successors Of Shivaji

Sambhaji (1680-89)
  • War of Succession was between Sambhaji the eldest son and Rajaram the youngest
  • Sambhaji ascended the throne in 1680
  • Placed his confidence in a Brahmin named Kanhoji, whom he gave supreme control of the administration and the title of Kavi Kalash
  • Provided protection and support to Akbar II the rebellious son of Aurangzeb
  • In 1689 Sambhaji and Kavi Kalash were captured by Mughal general Muqarrab Khan at Sangameshwar and put to death
  • Mughal army under Zulfiqar Khan launched an attack upon Raigarh, all the members of Sambhaji's family including his son Sahu were captured and imprisoned

Rajaram (1689-1700)
  • Rajaram proclaimed king by the Maratha council of ministers in 1689 in Raigarh
  • Apprehending a Mughal attack he fled to Jingi
  • Jingi was the capital and it remained the center of Maratha activity until 1698
  • He created the new post of Pratinidhi taking the number of ministers to nine
  • Jingi fell to the Mughals in 1689 and Rajaram escaped to Visalgarh, he died in March 1700 at Satara, which then became the capital of Maratha empire

Tarabai and Shivaji II (1700-07)
  • Tarabai, the widow of Rajaram put her son Shivaji II on the throne and herself became the de-facto ruler in 1700
  • During her reign the Marathas attaked Berar, Baroda and Aurangabad
  • Aurangzeb died on 3rd March 1707 while she was in power
  • The Mughals successfully divided the Marathas into two camps
    1. One under Tarabai
    2. One under Sambhaji's son Sahu
  • Sahu was successfull in deposing Tarabai with the help of Balaji Vishwanath

Sahu (1707-49)
  • Sahu was released by Mughal emperor Bahadur Shah, thus it was the begining of the civil war between Sahu and Tarabai
  • Tarabai's army was defeated in 1707 in the Battle of Khed, and Sahu occupied Satara
  • Maratha empire was divided into two
    • Shaivaji II or really Tarabai ruled from Kohlapur
    • Sahu ruled from Satara
  • After Shivaji II died, another son of Rajaram Sambhaji II ascended the throne of Kohlapur
  • The feud was settled by the Treaty of Warna in 1731
    • Sambhaji II to rule over southern division with Kohlapur as capital
    • Sahu to rule over the northen part with Satara as the capital
  • Sahu's reign saw the rise of Peshwaship

Maratha Power under the Peshwas

Balaji Vishwanath (1713-20)
  • Began his career as a Revenue Officer under Sahu in 1708
  • He was appointed as Peshwa in 1713
  • With his appointment as Peshwa, the title of Peshwa became hereditary, and the Peshwas became the de-facto rulers of the Maratha Empire
  • He played a crucial role in the civil war and was largely responsible for Sahu's win
  • He concluded an agreement with Sayyid Brothers by which the Mughal Empire Farrukhshiar recognised him as the king of the Swarajya and allowed him to Collect Cahuth and Sardeshmukhi from 6 Mughal province

Baji Rao I (1720-40)
  • Baji Rao I, the son of Balaji Vishwanath succeeded him at the age of 20
  • Was considered the greatest exponent of Guerilla warfare after Shivaji
  • His reign saw the begining of Maratha Confederacy and the rise of Maratha chiefs
  • Each Maratha chief was assigned a territory as his sphere of influence, which he was supposed to conquer on his own and rule autonomously
  • Consequently the prominent Maratha families were
    • Gaekwad at Baroda
    • Bhonsle at Nagpur
    • Holkars at Indore
    • Scindia at Gwalior
    • Peshwas at Poona
  • Defeated and expelled the Siddhis of Janjira from the mainland in 1722
  • Conquered Bassein and Salsette from the Portuguese in 1773
  • Defeated Nizam-Ul-Mulk and concluded the Treaty of Durai Sarai in 1737

Balaji Baji Rao (1740-61)
  • Popularly known as Nana Saheb
  • Agreement was reached between Mughal emperor Ahmed Shah and the Peshwa the later would protect the Mughal empire from internal and external enemies in exchange for Chauth of the North-Western provinces and total revenue of Agra and Ajmer
  • This brought Afgan invader Ahmed Shah Abdali and the Peshwa under direct conflict
  • Third battle of Panipat (1761) resulted in the defeat of the Marathas by Ahmed Shah Abdali
  • It also resulted in the death of Viswas Rao, the son of Nana Saheb, several other Maratha leaders and 28000 soldiers
  • Nana Saheb died on hearing the news on 23rd June 1761
