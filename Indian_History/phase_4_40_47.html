Struggle for Independence (1940-1947)

August Offer (1940)
  • Viceroy Linlithgow issued the statement on 8th August from Shimla
  • It was to ensure Congress support in WWII
  • The offer Turned down Congress demand for setting up National Government
  • It encouraged Anti-Congress organizations like Muslim League
  • Viceroy's Executive Council was enlarged from 7 to 12 members, of whom 4 British and 8 Indians
  • No Member of the Congress or the Muslim League joined the Executive Council
  • Congress rejected the August Offer
  • Immediate impact of the Offer was the launching of Individual Satyagraha

The Individual Satyagrahas
  • The August offer disillutioned the Congress
  • Gandhiji had a long meeting with the Viceroy At Shimla, September 1940
  • Gandhiji was convinced that the British would not modify their policy
  • On 17th October 1940 Acharya Vinoda Bhabe inaugurated the Satyagraha by delivering anti-war speeech at Paunar
  • Gandhiji suspended it on 17th December 1940 due to the little enthusiasm it created
  • Jawharlal Nehru was the second to offer Satyagraha
  • The Individual Satyagrahas was also known as Delhi Chalo Satyagraha

Cripps Mission, 1942
  • When WWII situation worsened, British Prime Minister Churchill was pressured to seek active support of Indians in the war
  • Cripps worked to keep India loyal to the British war effort in exchange for a promise of elections and full self-government (Dominion status) once the war was over.
  • Cripps offered Jinnah and the Muslims, the right to opt out of a future Union
  • The Muslim League therefore supported the war effort and gained in status in British eyes
  • Gandhji rightly described it as "An Invitation to the Muslim League to create Pakistan"
  • Gandhiji described the overall assessment as A post dated cheque on a crashing Bank
  • The League aslo finally rejected the Cripps Mission on the below grounds
    • It did not make seperate electorates
    • It did not have a clear cut acceptance for partition
  • The depressed classes and other minorities also wanted more safeguards for their communities
  • The British Government refused to accept the demand for immediate transfer of power

Quit India Movement, August 1942
  • Gandhiji came to beleive that Presence of the British in India is an invitation to the Japanese to invade, and British withdrawal removes the bait
  • July 14th 1942, the meeting of the COngress worling committee held at Wardha passed the Quit India resolution
  • Gandhiji asked the British to leave India to the hands of God
  • During the movement Gandhiji gave the slogan of Do or Die
  • The historic meeting took place at Gowalia Tank in Bombay, now known as August Kranti Maidan

Course of The Movement
  • On 9th August, police arrested Gandhi, Maulana Abul Kalam Azad and other prominent Congress leaders
  • From 9th to 13th August there was widespread chaos in different parts of the country, it is known as the Great August Uprising
  • Parallel Governments were set up in different Areas
    • Bengal (Tamluk/Midnapore) → Jayita Sarkar under Satish Samant
    • Orissa (Talcher) → Lakshman Nayak
    • Maharashtra (Satara) → Prati Sarkar under Nana Patil
    • Uttarpradesh (Baila) → Chittu Pandey, it was the longest running parallel government
  • It soon tok the shape of revolutionary activity led by leaders such as Achyut Patwardhan, Biju Patnaik, Sucheta Kriplani, RP Goenka etc.
  • Jayprakash Narayan, Ramnandan Mishra joined underground movement after escaoing from Hazaribagh jail on 9th November 1942
  • One of the most significant act was setting up of Congress Radio with Usha Mehta as the announcer

Responses and Trend
  • The movement got weak response in Punjab, Sind and NWFP
  • It got a massive response elsewhere in India
  • Congress was declared illegal
  • Muslim League stayed aloof from the movement
  • The Hindu Mahasabha, Communist Party of India opposed the movement
  • The Princes and landlords supported the British war effort

Repression by the Government
  • Government used planes to gun down people at various places, There were lathicharge floggings and imprisonment
  • Gandhiji commence fast on 10th February, 1934 in Jail. He declared it would be a 21 day fast
  • People secretly reached Poona to offer Satyagraha outside Agha Khan place where Gandhiji was being kept
  • The Ruler of Awadh, who was a pro Nationalist provided invaluable support to the by offering support to the Prati Sarkar activist

Subhas Chandra Bose and INA
  • Born in 1897
  • He was elected as the Mayor of Calcutta in 1932, while in jail
  • In 1939, he defeated Gandhiji's nominee Pattabhi Sitaramaya to be re elected Congress President
  • Subhas Chandra Bose founded the Forward Bloc in 1941
  • Rabindranath Tagore regarded him as Deshanayak

Indian National Army
  • Major Fujiwara, a Japanese officer persuaded Mohan Singh a prisoner of war to work in collaboration with the Japanese for India's freedom
  • Mohan Singh first conceived the idea of the INA in Malaya
  • In March 1942, a conference of Indians was held in Tokyo, and they formed the Indian Independence League, major role in its creation was played by Ras Behari Bose
  • In a conference in Bangkok (June 1942) Rashbehari was chosen as the President of the IIL
  • And in that same meeting a decision was taken to raise the Indian National Army or Azad Hind Fauz
  • Ras Behari resigned from the chairmanship of the IIL in favour of Subhash Chandra Bose who was named Supreme Commander of the INA
  • Bose returned to Singapore and formed the Provintial Government of free India Azad Hind Sarkar on 21st October 1943
  • In November 1943 the Japanese announced their decision to handover administration of Andaman and Nicobar islands to the INA
  • Bose renamed the islands to Shaheed and Swaraj
  • On March 1944, the INA commenced its military offence
  • Crossed the Indian Frontier on 18th March 1944
  • INA soldiers reached Kohima and captured it
  • However the INA failed to capture Imphal due to failure of Japanese to supply necessary material and air cover
  • Meanwhile, the status of war turned against Japan, and the British captured Rangoon in May 1946
  • INA troops were forced to surrender, Subhas supposedly died in a plane crash near Taiwan in August 1945
  • Bose set up two headquarters of the INA, in Rangoon and in Singapore
  • The INA had 3 fighting brigade namely Gandhi, Azad, Nehru
  • The women brigade of the INA was called Rani Jhansi brigade
  • Bose called Gandhiji Father of Nation
  • While Gandhiji returned the compliment by saying that Subhas was a prince among Patriots

INA Trials
  • Trial of INA soldiers began on 5th November 1945 at the histric red fort
  • Prominent INA officers to be put on trila were General Shah Nawaz, Gurdial Singh Dhillion, Prem Sehgal
  • The Congress declaring its support for the cause of the INA soldiers organised a INA Relief and Enquiry Committee
  • Tej Bahadur Sapru, KN Katju, Jawharlal Nehru and Asaf Ali took up the defence of the InA soldiers under the leadership of Bhulabhai Desai

Rajagopalachari Formula
  • Rajagopalachari had resigned from the Congess in 1943
  • Realised the necessity of a settlement between the Congress and the Muslim League in order to attain independence
  • He evolved a formula in 1944 to hold talks with Jinnah
  • Jinnah turned down the formula by saying it offered a Mutilated and moth-eaten Pakistan
  • But Jinnah agreed to hold talks with Gandhi
  • Jinnah demanded that the Muslims alone were to be entitled to vote for partition and not the whole population of the disputed areas
  • Gandhiji refused to accept this as it was based on the Two-Nation theory

Desai-Liaquat Proposals
  • Gandhiji directed Bhlabhai Desai to make another attempt to appease the league leaders and find a way put of the 1942-45 political impasse
  • Desai being a friend of Liaquat Ali (Deputy leader of the Muslim League), met him on January 1945
  • Liaquat Ali published a list of agreements
    • Equal number of persons of both sides in the Central Executive
    • Representation of the minorities especially of the Scheduled Castes and the Sikhs
    • Government was to be formed and was to function as per the existing Government of India Act of 1935

Wavell Plan, 1945
  • It was an attempt by Lord Wavell to resolve the political deadlock of 1945
  • He went to England for consultation
  • On 14th June 1945 the Viceroy orderd the release of all Congress working committee members
  • On this day he broadcast a plan known as Wavell Plan
  • He proposed the formation of a new Executive Council at the center which would consist of entirely Indian members including the Commander-in-chief
  • All portfolios Except Defence were to be under the control of Indian members
  • In the proposed council of 14 people, the Muslims were given a right to be over-represented with 6 members

Cabinet Mission Plan, 1946
  • Decision to send cabinet mission to India taken on 22nd January 1946
  • On 19th February 1946 British Prime Minister Clement Atlee announced in the House of Lords about the plan to quit India
  • High Powered Mission of 3 Cabinet Memberes reached Delhi on 24th March 1946
    • Sir Patric Lawrence → Secretary of State
    • Sir Stafford Cripps → President of Board of Trade
    • AV Alexander → First Lord of Admiralty
  • Purpose of the Mission
    • To find ways and means for the peaceful transfer of power in India
    • To suggest measures for the formation of a Constitution
    • To Set up Interim-Government

Recommendation of the Missions

Direct Action Day
  • The league decided on 30th July 1946, that 16th August would be observed as Direct Action day
  • Bengal Government led by HS Subhrawardy, declared August 16 to be a public holiday
  • The league organised public demostrations and Hartals, which escalated into clashes and rioting all over the city

Interim Government
  • Lord Wavell invited Jawharlal Nehru to form government on 12th August 1946
  • It finally came to being on 2nd September 1946
  • 13th October 1946 the Muslim league decided to join the government
  • 26th October, five nominees of the League joined the government. Congress appointees resigned to make room for them
  • Sarat Bose, Syed Ali Zahir, Sir Shafat Ahmed Khan resigned
