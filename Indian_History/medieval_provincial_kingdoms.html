Provincial Kingdoms

Vijayanagar Kingdom

Origins
  • Founded by Harihara I and Bukka
  • Originally in service of the Kakatiya ruler of Warangal Prataprudra II
  • Brought to the centre and converted to Islam by Mohammed Bin Tughlaq
  • Reconverted to Hindu under influence of Bhakti saint Vidyaranya
  • City of Vijayanagar established in 1336
  • Their main rivals were the Bahamani Sultans
  • Main area of contention was Raichur Doab (Between Krishna and TUngabhadra)

Vijayanagar Monarchy

The Sangama Dynasty
Harihara I (1336-56)
  • Founder of the Sangama dynasty
  • Took part on the confederacy organised by Krishna Nayak in 1344 to drive out the Muslims
Bukka I (1356-79)
  • Restored peace between warring Vaishnavs and Jains
  • Renamed city of Vidyanagar to Vijaynagar
Harihara II (1379-1404)
  • Took the titles of Parameshwara, Rajadhiraja
  • Embarked upon policy of expansion, leading the kingdom to new conflicts
Deva Raya I (1406-22)
  • Defeated by Bahamani ruler Firoz Shah
  • Italian traveller Nicolo De Conti visited in 1420
  • Srinath, his court poet wrote Haravilasam
  • There was a Pearl Hall to honor men of eminence
Deva Raya II (1422-46)
  • Wrote Mahanayaka Suddhanidhi
  • Took the title of Ganga Betekara (Elephant Hunter)
  • Abdur Razzaq the ambassador of Persian monarch Shah Rukh (Son of Timur) visited his court

The Suluva Dyansty (1486-1505)
  • Suluva Narasimha founded the dynasty in 1486
  • Succeeded by Tirumal and Immadi Narasimha in 1491
  • Vasco Da Gama landed in Calicut during his reign

The Tuluva Dynasty (1505-1570)
  • Founded by Vira Narasimha in 1505
  • Accorsing to Nuniz he was pious and distributed gifts at sacred places
Krishna Deva Raya
  • Greatest ruler of this dynasty
  • Ruled from 1509-29
  • Domingo Peas, Barbossa Albukirk, Frair Louis all came during his time
  • Golden age of Telugu literature
  • His political ideas are written in Amuktamalyada he also wrote Sanskrit drama Jambavati Kalyanam
  • Known as Andhra Pitamaha, Andhra Bhoja and Abhinava Bhoja
  • Eight great poets of telugu the Ashtadigwaja adorned his court
    • The Ashtadigwajas
    1. Allasani Peddanna → Manucharitam & Harikathasaramsamu
    2. Nandi Thimmana → Parijatapaharavam
    3. Madayyagari Mallana → Rajasekhara Charitam
    4. Dhurjaty → Kalahasti Mahatyam
    5. Ayyalaraju Ramambhadrudu → Sakalamatasara Sangraharr
    6. Pingali Surana → Raghava Pandaviyam & Prabhavati Pradyuman
    7. Ramarajabhushanudu → Kavvalankara Sangrahamu
    8. Tenali Ramakrishna → Panduranga Mahatyam
Sadashiva
  • Was the last ruler of this dynasty
  • Ceaser Frederick the Portuguese traveller visited his court
  • He was defeated in the Battle of Talikota (1565) by an alliance of Bujapur ,Golkonda and Bidar

The Aravidu Dynasty (1570-1652)
  • Ventaka II was the last great ruler
  • Raja Wodeyar founded the State of Mysore in 1612

Bahamani Kingdom

Hasan Gangu
  • Founded the kingdom in 1347 under the title of Alauddin Hasan Bahaman Shah

Mahmud Shah I
  • Son of Bahman Shah, fought with Vijayanagar empire

Firoz Shah (1397-1422)
  • Most remarkable ruler of the empire
  • Inducted Hindus in his administration
  • Built observatory in Daulatabad
  • Founded the city of Firozabad
  • Defeated Deva Raya I

Ahmed Shah Wali (1422-35)
  • Shifted capital from Gulbarga to Bidar
  • Known as Wali or saint due to his association with Gesu Daraz
  • Nikitin a Russian merchant visited during his reign
