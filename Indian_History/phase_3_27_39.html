Struggle for Independence (1927-1939)

Simon Commission
  • Lord Birkenhead Secretary of State for India, appointed a Seven Memberstatutory commission under chairmanship of John Simon
  • All the members were British and members of the British parliament
  • Appointed to review the performance of the reforms of 1919
  • Greeted with strong protest for having no Indian representative
  • Congress Session of 1927 in Madras resolved to Boycott it
  • The Muslim League was divided o this matter
    • Jinnah proposed to Boycott the commission
    • Whereas Muhammad Shafi did not oppose it
  • Justice Party in Madras, Unionist party in Punjab, Central Sikh Sangh also did not oppose the commission
  • The Commission came to India twice, once in 1928 and again in 1929, both time it faced boycott

Nehru Report
  • In response to the Simon commission, all parties conference was called on 28th February 1928 in Delhi
  • It was presided over by MA Ansari in Bombay on 19th May
  • The all parties conference appointed a commission led by Motilal Nehru
  • The aim of the commission was to determine the principles of the Constitution of India
  • The Committee consisted of TB Sapru, Sir Ali Iman, MS Aney, NM Joshi, Mangal Singh, Subhas Chandra Bose

Recommendation of the Report
  • India must be given dominion status
  • India would be a federation having bicameral legislature
  • No provision for seperate electorate
  • Citizenship was defined and fundamental rights were enunciated
  • Annual Session of the INC at Calcutta in 1928, approved Nehru report and served ultimatum on the British government to accept the report within 31st Deember 1929
  • The open session of the Muslim league at Delhi on 28th March 1929 rejected the Nehru report and accepted Mr. Jinnah's 14 points

Jinnah's 14 Points
  • Announced by Jinnah in Delhi on 28th March 1929
  • Minimum one-third muslim representation in the central as well as provintial legislature
  • Representation of communal groups to continue by seperate electorates
  • Territorial re-distribution should not affect the muslim majority of Punjab and Bengal

Lahore Session of Congress (1929)
  • Jawharlal Nehru was the president of this historic session
  • Poorna Swaraj resolution was passed
  • Future elections was to be boycotted
  • A programme of Civil Disobedience was to be launched
  • Gandhi was to determin the time, place and issue on which CDM was to be launched
  • On 31st December 1929 Jawharlal Nehru, the president of INC unfurled the flag of India's independence on the banks of the Ravi river in Lahore
  • Congress working committee which met of 2nd January 1930, decided to observe 26th January 1930 as Poorna Swaraj Day

Civil Disobedience Movement

First Phase

Gandhiji's Demands
  • Gandhiji placed ultimatum on 31st January 1930 to the Government for the following demands
  • Reduction of Rupee Sterling ratio
  • Abolition of salt tax, and government salt monopoly
  • Release of all political prisoners
  • Change in arms act allowing citizens to bear arms for self-protection

Dandi March
  • On 12th March 1930 Gandhiji started historic march with 78 followers
  • March was from Sabarmati Ashram to Dandi
  • After 24 day long march he broke the salt law at Dandi on 5th April 1930
  • In Tamilnadu C Gopalachari led salt march from Trichinopolly to Vedaranniyam on Tanjore coast
  • In Malabar K Kelappan walked from Calicut to Payynur to break the salt law

Course of the Movement
  • Chittagong armoury raid and battle on Jalalabad hill on 22nd April 1930 under the able leadership of Surya Sen
  • In United province and Gujarat No Tax campaign was launched on 18th April 1930
  • The Viceroy took the initiative of releasing Congress leaders and invited Mahatma Gandhi for talks

Gandhi-Irwin Pact
  • First round table conference was called in London in November 1930 to consider the recommendation of the Simon Commission
  • Gandhiji initiated talk with Irwin on 14th February, 1931, it culminated in Delhi Pact of 5th March 1931
  • Congress agreed to participate in the Second Round Table Conference
  • The Government agreed to release all Political prisoners and restore the confiscated property of the Satyagrahis
  • Gandhiji requested for remitting the death sentence on Bhagat Singh, Sukhdev and Rajguru, it was turned down by the Viceroy

Second Phase
  • Second Round Table conference (September 1931) failed to satisfy the INC
  • Gandhiji returned to India disheartened and disillutioned, Lord Wellington who succeeded Irwin, flouted many provisions of the Gandhi-Irwin pact
  • On 4th January 1932 fresh batch of Congress leaders were arrested including Gandhiji and Sardar Patel
  • Gandhiji was placed in Yervada prison
  • Amidst announcement of Communal Award by Ramsay McDonald on 16th August 1932 and Gandhijis fast until death, the Civil disobediance movement lost its momentum
  • Gandhiji lost interest in the Civil disobedience movement, and got involved in anti-untouchability struggle, which led to the formation of Harijan Sevak Sangha
  • Gandhiji launched Individual Civil Disobedience on 1st August 1933

Round Table Conferences

First Round Table Conference
  • John Simon recommended to call the meeting to take decision on the issue of constitutional reforms
  • Organization Leader
    Hindu Mahasabha MR Jayakar, MS Moonje
    Sikh Sardar Sampurna Singh
    Christians KT Paul
    Liberals TB Sapru, CY Chintamani, Srinivas Shastri
    Muslim League Aga Khan, Jinnah, Mohammed Shafi
    Depressed Class BR Ambedkar
    Princes Akbar Hydari, Mirza Ismail, Maharaja of Bikaner

Second Round Table Conference
  • Wellington succeeded Lord Irwin in April 1931
  • Sir Samuel Hoare a leading conservative became Secretary of State for India
  • Congress suspended Civil Disobedience movement, but reiterated Poorna Swaraj as its ultimate goal
  • Organization Leader
    Congress Gandhiji
    Liberals TB Sapru
    Muslim League Mohammed Iqbal
    Depressed Class BR Ambedkar
    Capitalist GD Birla
    Others Sarojini Naidu, MM Malviya
  • It was decided that responsible government would be established immediately, including complete control over finance, army and external relations

Third Round Table Conference
  • Called on 17th November 1932
  • Boycotted by Congress
  • Prominent members were TB Sapru and Ambedkar
  • On the basis of this discussion proposals were drafted for the reforms of the Constitution
  • Embodied in White paper published in March 1933
  • A bill based on this paper was passed in the British Parliament as Government of India Act 1935

Communal Award and Poona Pact
  • British Prime Minister Ramsay MacDonald proposed minority representation method known as Communal Award on 16th August 1932
  • It reserved number of seats in the Provintial Legislature for the minority
  • According to this award Muslims, Sikhs, Anglo-Indians, Christians etc would elect their candidates by voting in seperate communal electorates
  • Gandhiji reacted strongly to this proposal and wrote to the British Prime Minister on 18th August 1932
  • He went on fast unto death from 20th September 1932, this day was observed as a day of prayer. Temples and wells were thrown open to the people of the depressed classes
  • On 25th September Poona Pact was signed to sort out the situation
    • Principle of Joint and Common electorate was accepted
    • 48 seats in different provintial legislatures would be reserved for the depressed classes
    • Adequete representation would be given to depressed class in civil services

Election of 1937
  • In Lucknow session of 1936, Presided by Jawharlal Nehru Congress resolved to contest elections
  • Nehru advocated Socialism and took three socialists into the Congress working committee JayPrakash Narayan, Acharya Narendra Deo, Achyut Patwardhan
  • During the Lucknow Session, the first Session of All India Kisan Sabha was held under the presidentship of Swami Sajahanand Saraswati
  • Congress obtained clear majority in Madras, United Province, Central Province, Bihar and Orissa
  • In Bengal, Punjab and Sind Congress did not have majority
