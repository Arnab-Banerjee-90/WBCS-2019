Mauryan Age (321-184 B.C)

Origins of the Mauryans
  • Junagarh Inscription (150 A.D) → suggests that the Mauryas might have been of Vaishya orogin
  • Mudrarakshas (Visakhadutta) → Mauryas were connected to the Nandas and belonged to a lower caste (Vrishala/Kulhina)
  • Buddhist Traditions → Chandragupta Maurya was of Kshatriya origin and the region was full of Mors (peacock) so the name 'Moriya'
  • Puranas → They belonged to the Moriya clan (low caste)

Sources
  • Arthashastra (Kautilya/Chanakya) → Treatise on state craft and public admistration, written in sanskrit
  • Indica (Megasthenes) → Socio economic conditions during the Maurya period and the administrative, caste system
  • Ashoka Vandana and Divya Vandana → Information about Bindusara and Ashoka's expedition to Taxila
  • Dipvamsa and Mahavamsa → Ashoka's role in spreading Buddhism in Sri Lanka
  • Jatakas → Socio economic conditions during the Maurya period
  • Puranas → Chronology of the Maurya kings

Chandragupta Maurya
  • Defeated Dhanananda with the help of Chanakya/Kautilya also known as Vishnugupta
  • Called Sandrocottus or Androcottus by the Greeks
  • Defeated Selucus Nikator, the general of Alexander in North-West india in 305 B.C.Selucus surrendered territory in return of 500 elephants. Hindukush become the boundary between the two states
  • Selucus also sent Greek Ambassador Megasthenes to his court
  • His brother-in-law Pushyagupta (Simanta Raja) constructed Sudarsana Lake
  • Patronised the first Jaina Council at Pataliputra in the year 300 B.C
  • He embraced Jainism and went to Chandagiri hills in Sravanbelagola with Bhadrabahu, where he died of slow starvation (Sallekhana)
  • First Indian ruler to unite the entire North India

Bindusara (298 - 273 B.C)
  • Called Amrito Chastes (Amitraghat) by the Greeks
  • Extended his kingdom to the peninsular region of India as far as Mysore
  • Taranath a Buddhist scholar credits him for conquering the land between the two seas.
  • Antiochus I (King of Syria) sent his ambassador Deimachus to his court
  • Ptolemy Philadelphius (King of Egypt) sent his ambassador Dionysius

Ashoka (273 - 232 B.C)
  • According to Dipavamsa Ashoka usurped the throne after killing his 99 brothers exept youngest one Tissa
  • Fought in the Kalinga war in 261 B.C
  • The misery of war caused deep remorse in Ashoka and and he abandoned his policy of physical conquest (Bherighosa) in favour of cultural conquest (Dhammaghosha)
  • Embraced Buddhism under Upagupta
  • Convened the Third Buddhist Council in 250 B.C at Pataliputra
  • Sent his son Mahendra and daughter Snaghamitra to Ceylon as Buddhist missionaries
  • Inaugurated Dhammayatras from 11th year of his reogn by visiting Bodh-Gaya, also appointed Dhamma Mahatmas (officers of righteousness)
  • He was the first ruler to issue edicts (order/command)

Minor Rock Edicts
  • Concentrated in the SOuth and central part of the empire
  • Highlight Ashoka's activity as a Buddhist disciple
  • Most commonly adopted title in edicts is Devanam Piyadassi

Pillar Edicts
  • Mainly found in Delhi-Topra, Delhi-Meerut, Ram-Purva, Allahabad-Kosam
  • Ashokan Allahabad edict contains two later inscriptions by Samudragupta and Jahangir
  • Pillar edict VII is the last edict issued by Ashoka

Later Mauryas
  • Following Ashoka's death the Mauryan dynasty lasted for another 137 years
  • Brihadhrata was the last Mauryan king assassinated by his Brahmin commander in-chief Pushyamitra Shunga in 184 B.C to establish the Shunga dynasty
