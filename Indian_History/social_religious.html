Social and Religious Reforms

Hindu Socio-Religious Movement

Young Bengal Movement
  • The Young Bengal movement was a group of radical Bengali free thinkers emerging from Hindu College, Calcutta. They were also known as Derozians, after their firebrand teacher at Hindu College, Henry Louis Vivian Derozio
  • Derozio taught in the Hindu College from 1826-31
  • He took his inspiration from the French revolution
  • He was dismissed because of his radical ideas
  • He edited the newspaper Bengal Gazette and Calcutta Gazette

Brahmo Samaj
  • Rammohan Roy founded the Atmiya Sabha in 1815-19
  • In 1828 he established the Brahmo Sabha and later the Brahmo Samaj
  • This society believed that God it the cause and source of all that exists
  • It did not recognise any socially priviledged class as mediataries between man and God
  • Roy and his associates faced bitter enimity and ridicule from orthodox hindus
  • In 1866 there was a aplit in the Brahmo Sabha
  • Devendra Nath Tagore founded Adi Brahmo Samaj and Tattobodhoni Sabha
  • Keshab Chandra Sen founded Brahmo Samaj of India
  • Ananda Mohan Bose strated the Sadharan Brahmo Samaj

Dharma Sabha
  • Established by Radhakant Dev in 1830
  • Its main purpose was to conseve the orthodox nature of hinduism
  • It countered Brahmo Samaj

Manav Dharma Sabha
  • one of the earliest socio-religious reform movements in Gujarat and British India
  • Formed in 1844
  • Founded by Mehtaji Durgaram Manchharam, Dadoba Pandurang Tarkhadkar

Paramhansa Mandali
  • Founded by Dadoba Pandurang and Bal Shastri Jambedkar in 1849
  • Believed in one god

Prarthana Samaj
  • In 1867, the Prarthana Samaj was formed by Atmaram Pandurang
  • Its aim was anit-caste movement and women upliftment
  • Its two main architect were MG Ranade and Ramkrishna Bhandarkar
  • Ranade was a great advocate of Hindu-Muslim unity

GH Deshmukh and Jyotiba Phule
  • They were two great reformers in Western India
  • Gopal Hari Deshmukh was popularly known asLokhitwadi
  • He supported reorganization of society on the principle of modernity, secularism and humanism
  • Mahatma Phule dedicated himself for the couse of the opressed sections of society and women upliftment
  • in 1873 Jyotiba Phule established the Sastyoshodhak Samaj which was open to everyone without any distinction to caste and religion

Arya Samaj
  • Founded in Bombay, by Dayanand Saraswati in 1875
  • He looked at the Vedas as the true seed of Hinduism
  • His motto was 'India for Indians'
  • He said 'Go back to the Vedas'
  • Satyarth Prakash was the most important book written by him
  • He also wrote Veda-Bhasya and Veda-Bhasya Bhumika
  • He tried to reconvert those Hindus who had been converted to Islam or Christianity
  • For this purpose a purificationary ceremony called Shuddhi was organised by him

Theosophical Movement
  • Founded by Madam HP Blavatsky, Colonel Olcott in 1875 in New York
  • Theosophical Society of India was founded by Annie Besant
  • She founded Central Hindu College in 1898 which later became Benaras Hindu University in 1916

Deccan Educational Society
  • Founded in 1884 by MG Ranade, GG Agarkar
  • Objective was to contribute to the education of Western India

Seva Sadan
  • Founded by Behramji M Malabari in 1885
  • Campaign against child marriage
  • Care for socially exploited women

Indian National Social Conference
  • Founded by MG Ranade in 1887
  • Focus was to abolish Kulinism and polygamy
  • Also referred to as the Social reform Cell of the INC

Ramakrishna Mission
  • Founded in 1897 at Belur Math near Calcutta by Narendranath Dutta
  • It was to spread the Teachings of Ramakrishna Pramhansa (1836-86)
  • It stood for the social service 'The best way to serve God is to serve mankind'
  • Vivekanada took part in the Parliment of Religions in 1893 in Chicago USA
  • His adress made a deep impression on the people of other countries, thus helping to raise the prestige of Indian culture

Muslim Socio-Religious Movement

Wahabi Movement
  • Earliest organised Muslim response to western influences
  • Revivalist movement inspired by the teachings of Shah Waliullah (1702-62)
  • Was mainly targeted towards the Sikhs in Punjab
  • After annexation of Punjab in 1849 it was also targeted towards the British
  • Suppressed in 1870

Titu Mir's Movement
  • Syed Mir Nisar Ali Titumir was a disciple of Syed Ahmed of Rai Bairelly, founder of the Wahabi movement
  • He organized muslim peasants against the Hindu zamindars and the British Indigo planters
  • He was killed in action in 1831

Fairazi Movement
  • Founded by Haji Shariat-Allah
  • Under the leadership of his son Dadu Mian it became revolutionalry from 1840
  • Mostly popular in Faridpur East Bengal
  • It aimed to rid the Muslim society of non-Islamic customs

Deoband Movement
  • Founded in 1867 by Mohammad Qasim Nanotavi and Rashid Ahmed Gangohi
  • Centered at Deoband, Shaharanpur UP
  • Aim was promotion of calssical Islamic studies, and was against Western education and culture
  • Supported the Indian National Congress and opposed the Aligarh Movement

Aligarh Movement
  • Pioneered by Syed Ahmed Khan
  • Its aim was liberalization of Indian Islam
  • He advocated English education for the regeneration of Mulsims in India
  • In 1864 he established the Translation society which translated English books on science into Urdu
  • It was later renamed to Scientific Society
  • He established Mohameddan Anglo Oriental College in 1875
  • It later came to be knoen as Aligarh Muslim University
  • Syed Ahmed opposed the activities of Indian National Congress
  • He wanter more time for the Indian Mulsims to organise and consolidate their position,a nd though it could be done by maintaining good relations with the British rulers

Ahmaddiya Movement
  • Founded by Mirza Ghulam Ahmed in 1899
  • It was like the Muslim equivalent of Brahmo Samaj
  • The movement spread Western liberal education among the Indian Muslims
