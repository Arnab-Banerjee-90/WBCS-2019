Post Mauryan Empires (185 B.C - 319 C.E)

Indegenous Ruling Dynasties

Shunga Dynasty (185-75 B.C)
  • Information about Shunga dynasty found from Harshacharit (Banbhatta), Mahavasya (Patanjali), Malavikagnimitram (Kalidasa)
  • Shunga empire confined to Magadha and central India only
  • Pushyamitra Shunga was the first king, assassinated Brihadratha the last Mauryan king
  • He Ruled from Vidisha
  • He is known as prosecutor of Buddhism
  • Greek ambassador Heliodorus visited court of fifth Shunga king Bhagabhadra
  • Heliodorus constructed a pliiar calledGarudadhawaja in honor of Vishnu
  • Debabhuti was the last Shunga king, he was assassinated by his Brahmin minister Vasudeva

Kanva Dynasty (75-30 B.C)
  • Vasudeva was the founder
  • Mainly centered in Magadha
  • Succeeded by Bhumimitra, Narayana and Susharman
  • Last ruler Susarman, killed by Andhra (Satvahana) king Simuka

    • Chedi Dynasty
  • Capital was Kalinganagara
  • Greatest ruler was Kharvela
  • Only source of information is the Hatigumpha inscription in Prakit and Brahmi

    • Satvahana Dynasty
  • Successors of Mauryas in Deccan
  • Capital was Paithan and Amaravati
  • Language in use was Prakit
  • Simuka was the founder of this empire
  • His successor Krishna ruled over the Satavahana Kingdom for eighteen Years
  • After him, Sri Satakarni ascended the throne
  • He was a powerful king and took the title of Dakshinpatha-pati after performing Ashwamedha and Rajasuya yagnas
  • His deeds are known from Nanghat Inscription by his queen Nayanika
  • He was attacked and defeated by Kalinga king Kharvela
  • Gautamiputra Satakarni revived the Satvahana dynasty, he was the greatest Satvahana ruler
  • His deeds are known from the Nashik inscription by his mother Gautami Balashree
  • He defeated Saka ruler Nahapana but was defeated by Saka ruler Rudradaman
  • He married his son Basistha putra Satkarni to the daughter of Rudradaman to end the hostilities with the Sakas
  • Last great king was Yajna Sri Satkarni
  • Last Satvahana king of the main line was Pulamai VI

    • Foreign Ruling Dynasties

    Indo-Greeks
    • Greek expansion started by Demetrius I
    • Menander was the greatest Indo-Greek ruler
    • Sakala (present Sialkot) was his capital
    • Wrote Milindapanho which is in the form of a dialogue between Milinda and Nagasena

    Sythians
    • Bhumaka was the first ruler
    • Succeeded by Nahapana
    • His power was crushed by Gautamiputra Satkarni

    Shakas (BC 90-100 AD)
  • Rudradaman was the greatest king
  • Junagarh inscription testifies that he twice defeated deccan ruler Satkarni
  • Mentions repair of Sudarshana Lake by his governor in Shaurashtra

    • Parthians
    • Gondophernes was the greatest ruler
    • Ruled from AD 19-45 based on Takth-i-Bahi inscription

    Kushans
    • Kujula Kodphises I was the first ruler
    • Issued copper coins which showed Roman influence
    • Succeeded by Vima Kodphises
    • Also known as Yen Kao Chan
    • First King to introduce Gold Coins
    • Succeeded by Kanishka
    • kanishka was the greatest ruler of this dynasty
    • Ascended the throne on AD 78, this year is the starting of Sakabda (Sakakala)
    • Ruled from Purushpur (Peshawar)
    • Nagarjuna, Ashwaghosha, Parsva and Vasumitra was in his court
    • Charaka was his court physician
    • Succeeded by Vashishka and then Huvishka
    • Vasudeva was the last king of this dynasty
    • Assumed the title of Shaono Shao Vasudevo Koshana
