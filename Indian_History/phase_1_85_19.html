Struggle for Independence (1885-1919)

Moderates (1885-1905)

Demands of the Moderates
  • Removal of restriction of freedom of press and freedom of speech
  • Seperation of judiciary from executive
  • Claim of self government within the British empire, Gokhale first made this demand in 1905, floowed by Dadabhai Naoroji in 1906
  • Demeanded the end of Economic drain of India
  • Abolition of discriminatory laws
  • Oppertunities of Indians in administrative jobs
  • Abolition of salt tax, reimposition of import duties

Acheivements of the Moderates
  • Nation wide awakening of nationalism
  • Exposed expolitory nature of British rule, 'Theory of drain of welath' played a vital role in this regard
  • Appointment of public service commission in 1886, enactment of Indian Councils Act of 1892, it gave some powers to the elected local bodies
  • Resolution in house of commons in 1893 for Simultaneous ICS examination in England and India
  • Welby Commission on Indian expenditure in 1895

The Extemists (1905-1919)

Cause of Rise of Extremists
  • "Poverty and Unbritish Rule" - Dadabhai Naoroji
  • Ranaday's essay on Indian Economy
  • "Economic History of India" - RC Dutt
  • Deportation of Chapekar brothers without trial in 1897
  • Calcutta Corporation act 1899
  • Indian Universities act 1904
  • Indian Official Secrets Act 1904
  • Reactionary rule of Lord Curzon (1889-1905)
  • Partition of Bengal 1905

Prominent Extremists

Bal Gangadhar Tilak (1856-1920)
  • Lokmanya to the Indians and Father of Indian Unrest to the British
  • He used religious orthodoxy as a method of mass contact
  • Organised Ganapati (1893) and Shivaji (1896) festival
  • He was the first to give slogan about Swaraj
  • Distinguished member of the Deccan education society
  • He also editd Maratha and Keshari
  • Played a prominent role in Anti-Partition movement and was responsible for making it an all India movement

Lala Lajpat Rai (1865-1928)
  • Known as Punjab Keshari
  • He was the leader of the college faction of the Arya Samaj
  • Editor of Punajbi and authored a book called Unhappy India
  • The cult of Swadeshi was propagated in Punjab by Lala Lajpat Rai
  • While leading Anti-Simon procession he was wounded ant later died of injuries

Bipin Chandra Pal (1858-1932)
  • Known as Father of Revolutionary Thought
  • Started New India in 1901 to propagate his brand of Nationalism revolving around the ideas of Boycott, Swaraj and National education
  • He began his career as a moderate, but later drifted toward extremism

Aurobindo Ghosh (1872-1950)
  • Advocated the doctrine of passive resistance
  • Published New Lamps For Old in 1893-94 criticising the moderate politics of INC
  • Played an important roll in the Anti-Partition movement and proposed the theory of relentless boycott
  • In 1910 he went to Pondicherry and threafter concentrated on Philosophical, spiritual and literary activities
  • Some of his books were Life Divine, Savitri

Partition of Bengal

British Idea
  • Idea first put forward by William Ward, governor of Assam in 1896
  • Curzon said "The Indians only business was to be governed and it was sacrilege on its part to have any other aspiration"
  • Partition between Hindus and Muslims

Road to Partition
  • Viceroy wrote his minute on Territorial Redistribution on 1st June 1903
  • The province came into existence on 16th October 1905
  • Two States were born, namely Bengal and East Bengal

Swadeshi Movement
  • Aim was the annulment of the Partition, but it was Superceded by greater objective of attainment of Swaraj
  • Aurobindo Ghosh came out with theory of relentless Boycott of everything British
  • Emphasis was given on Atmashakti (Rabindranath Tagore)
  • Industrial ventures set us such as
    • Bengal Chemicals
    • Calcutta Potteries
    • Lakshmi Cottons
    • Mohini Mills
    • National Tannery
  • To suppress the students Carlyle Circuler of 22nd October 1905 was issued, which threatened to withdraw grants, scholerships and affiliations of the institutions
  • In 1905 session of INC at Benaras, Congress supported the movement only in Bengal, and refused to accept the demands of the extremists to extend the movement
  • In 1906 Session of INC at Calcutta, Dadabhai Naoroji declared the aim of the Congress to be Swaraj (Self Government)
  • Indian society of Oriental Art set up in 1907, Nandalal Bose first recepient of scolarship
  • Large number of volunteer groups sprang up
    • Dawn society by Sachindra Mukherjee
    • Anti-Circular Society
    • The Anushilan by Pramotha Mitra
    • Dacca Anushilan Samita by Pulin Das
  • Many prominent muslims joined the movement such as Abdul Rasul ,Liaqat Hussain, Maulana Abul Kalam Azad
  • With split of the INC in 1907 in Surat the movement lost its major strength

Annulment of the Partition
  • In December 1911 King George V and the Queen Empress visited India (Only King to Visit India)
  • on 12th December 1911 a magnificient Coronation Durbar was held in Delhi
  • Official Proclamation regarding annulment of Bengal Partition and transfer of British capital from Calcutta to Delhi was made at the Durbar

Hardinge Bomb Case
  • 23rd December 1912 was fixed date for Vicreoy's entry into the new capital
  • Bomb was thrown by Rash Behari Bose at the elephant carrying the viceroy
  • The Viceroy was badly injured
  • Bhai Bhai Mukund was sentenced to death in the Hardinge case

Surat Split
  • Difference between the moderates and extremeists widened during the Swadeshi movement
  • Issues being Spread of the Movement outside Bengal, Choice of the method of struggle, clash of personalities etc.
  • The gowing differences came to the fore in the Surat Session of the INC in 1907
  • Rash Behari Ghosh was chosen as the president, against the wishes of the extremists who wanted Lala Lajpat Rai as the president
  • The moderates were in the majority, and the extremists were suspended from the INC

All India Muslim League
  • The Partition of Bengal in 1905 created a communal divide
  • In October 1 1906, a Muslim deputation led by Agha Khan, met Lord Minto at Shimla, it is called the Shimla Deputation
  • The deputation claimed a priviledged position of Indian Muslims
  • Minto gave categorical assurance that the political rights and interestes of the Muslim community would be safeguarded
  • The Simla deputation was followed in Eastern Bengal by muslim meetings in favour of the partition
  • Nawab Habibullah (or Salimullah) of Dacca was the leader of such meetings in Dacca
  • On 30th December 1906 a political association called All India Muslim League was formed

Demand for a Separate Electorate
  • The demand for seperate electorate for Muslims was made in the Amritsar Session of the league in 1908 under presidentship of Sir Syed Ali Iman
  • Their demends were conceded in the form of Morley-Minto reforms in 1909
  • Jinnah in the Allahabad session of INC in 1910 moved a resolution condemning the system of communal elctorates

Home Rule Movement, 1916
  • The term 'Home Rule' was borrowed from a similar movement in Ireland
  • BG Tilak and Annie Besant were the pioneers
  • Aim of the movement was to attain home-rule for India within the British empire, on the lines of the autonomous colonies of Australia and New Zealand

Tilak's Home Rule League
  • Launched at Bombay provintial conference in Belgaum in April 1916
  • It was organised into 6 branches
  • It published pamphlets in regional languages and in English
  • Demands included Swaraj, formation of Linguistic states, education in Vernacular languages
  • During this movement Tilak was given the title of Lokmanya

Aniie Besant's Home Rule League
  • She has come from England to India in 1893 to work for the Theosophical society
  • Her headquarters was in Adyar near Madras
  • She started her Home Rule in September 1916, in Madras
  • George Arundale was the organising secretary
  • She brought out papers Commonwealth, New India
  • Main aim was to popularise the idea of and achieving self government
  • Mrs. Besant finally dropped her league after Montague's Declaration in 1917

Lucknow Pact
  • In the Lucknow session of 1916 both the INC and the Muslim League reached an agreement to cooperate in the political field on the basis of a common programme, this is referred to as the Lucknow Pact
  • The extremists were readmitted into the INC
  • BG Tilak and Annie Besant dominated the session
  • Exhorted the British government to confer self-governement as soon as possible
  • It demanded greater representation of the elected members of the expanded council
  • This pact also marked the formal acceptance of seperate electortes for Muslims
  • All-India Hindu Mahasabha led crusade against the Lucknow pact
  • The pact succeeded in getting the reforms through Montague-Chelmsford reforms
  • The pact lasted till the suspension of the Non-cooperation movement in February 1922, after the Chauri-Chaura incident

Second Split
  • Reactions within the INC regarding the Government of India Act of 1919 varied sharply
  • Moderates welcomed it as the Magna-Carta of India
  • Extremists criticised it for falling short of the legitimate expectations of India
  • The INC in a special session in 1918 critisesed the declaration as dissapointing and suggested important modifications
  • The moderates led by Surendranath Banerjee supported the declaration in a seperate conference in 1918
  • This brought about the second spilit in the INC, with the ultra-moderates starting a new party called National Liberation League which later came to be known as All-India Liberal Federation

Revolutionary Activities

First Phase
  • Basudev Balwant Phadke known as Father of Millitant Nationalism
  • He gathered backward classes of Kols and Bhils and tried to create rebellion within the empire, he was captured and got deported to Aden in1902
  • The Revolutionary groups set up were
    • Midnapore Society by Sarla Ghosal
    • Yugantar group by Barindra Kumar Ghosh, Raja Subodh Mallick and Hemchandra Quanungo
  • First political bank robbery known as Rangpur Dacoiti in 1906
  • In 1908 Yugantar group tried to murder Kingsford, the Magistrate of Muzzaffarpur, but failed
  • In Madras revelutionary activities were carried out by Bharat Mata Association under Vanchi Iyer
  • India House by Shyamaji Krishna Verma in London
  • Abhinava Bharat and Mitra Mela by VD savarkar
  • Paris Indian Society set up by Madam Bhikaji Cama
  • She also started two newspapers Vande Mataram and Madans Talwar
  • Indian Independence Committee set up by Virendranath Chattopadhyay in Berlin
  • Ghadar Party Movement set up by Hardayal Singh in San Francisco in 1913
  • They published the newspaper called Hindustani Ghadar
  • The Ghadar movement was carried out in Punjab by Kartar Singh and in Hong-Kong by Bhagwan Singh
  • The Ghadar party movement ended with the arrest of Harldayal, and Komagata Incident

Second Phase
  • Naujawan Bharat Sabha founded by Bhagat Singh
  • Murder of Saunders, Bhagat Singh was accused
  • Assembly Bomb case (1929), Bahgat Singh, Batukeshwar Dutt and Rajguru
  • Chittagong Armory Dacoity, Surya Sen (1930)
  • General Dyer Murdered by Udham Singh in London in 1940
