Struggle for Independence (1885-1919)

Moderates (1885-1905)

Demands of the Moderates
  • Removal of restriction of freedom of press and freedom of speech
  • Seperation of judiciary from executive
  • Claim of self government within the British empire, Gokhale first made this demand in 1905, floowed by Dadabhai Naoroji in 1906
  • Demeanded the end of Economic drain of India
  • Abolition of discriminatory laws
  • Oppertunities of Indians in administrative jobs
  • Abolition of salt tax, reimposition of import duties

Acheivements of the Moderates
  • Nation wide awakening of nationalism
  • Exposed expolitory nature of British rule, 'Theory of drain of welath' played a vital role in this regard
  • Appointment of public service commission in 1886, enactment of Indian Councils Act of 1892, it gave some powers to the elected local bodies
  • Resolution in house of commons in 1893 for Simultaneous ICS examination in England and India
  • Welby Commission on Indian expenditure in 1895

The Extemists (1905-1919)

Cause of Rise of Extremists
  • "Poverty and Unbritish Rule" - Dadabhai Naoroji
  • Ranaday's essay on Indian Economy
  • "Economic History of India" - RC Dutt
  • Deportation of Chapekar brothers without trial in 1897
  • Calcutta Corporation act 1899
  • Indian Universities act 1904
  • Indian Official Secrets Act 1904
  • Reactionary rule of Lord Curzon (1889-1905)
  • Partition of Bengal 1905

Prominent Extremists

Partition of Bengal

British Idea
  • Idea first put forward by William Ward, governor of Assam in 1896
  • Curzon said "The Indians only business was to be governed and it was sacrilege on its part to have any other aspiration"
  • Partition between Hindus and Muslims

Road to Partition
  • Viceroy wrote his minute on Territorial Redistribution on 1st June 1903
  • The province came into existence on 16th October 1905
  • Two States were born, namely Bengal and East Bengal

Swadeshi Movement
  • Aim was the annulment of the Partition, but it was Superceded by greater objective of attainment of Swaraj
  • Aurobindo Ghosh came out with theory of relentless Boycott of everything British
  • Emphasis was given on Atmashakti (Rabindranath Tagore)
  • Industrial ventures set us such as
    • Bengal Chemicals
    • Calcutta Potteries
    • Lakshmi Cottons
    • Mohini Mills
    • National Tannery
  • To suppress the students Carlyle Circuler of 22nd October 1905 was issued, which threatened to withdraw grants, scholerships and affiliations of the institutions
  • In 1905 session of INC at Benaras, Congress supported the movement only in Bengal, and refused to accept the demands of the extremists to extend the movement
  • In 1906 Session of INC at Calcutta, Dadabhai Naoroji declared the aim of the Congress to be Swaraj (Self Government)
  • Indian society of Oriental Art set up in 1907, Nandalal Bose first recepient of scolarship
  • Large number of volunteer groups sprang up
    • Dawn society by Sachindra Mukherjee
    • Anti-Circular Society
    • The Anushilan by Pramotha Mitra
    • Dacca Anushilan Samita by Pulin Das
  • May prominent muslims joined the movement such as Abdul Rasul ,Liaqat Hussain, Maulana Abul Kalam Azad
  • With split of the INC in 1907 in Surat the movement lost its major strength

Annulment of the Partition
  • In December 1911 King George V and the Queen Empress visited India (Only King to Visit India)
  • on 12th December 1911 a magnificient Coronation Durbar was held in Delhi
  • Official Proclamation regarding annulment of Bengal Partition and transfer of British capital from Calcutta to Delhi was made at the Durbar

Hardinge Bomb Case
  • 23rd December 1912 was fixed date for Viceoy's entry into the new capital
  • Bomb was thrown by Rash Behari Bose at the elephant carrying the viceroy
  • The Viceroy was badly injured
  • Bhai Bhai Mukund was sentenced to death in the Hardinge case
