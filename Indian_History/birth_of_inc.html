Birth of Indian National Congress

Background
  • Credit for establishment of the INC is given to AO Hume
  • He was a retired British member of the Indian Civil Services
  • Earlier he founded the Indian National Union in 1884, said to be the forerunner to the INC
  • He served as the General Secretary of the INC from 1885-1906

Formation
  • Formed on 28th December 1885
  • First session of the INC was held at Tejpal Sanskrit Pathshala, Bombay
  • President of the first session was WC Banerjee
  • This session was attended by 72 delegates
  • In 1889 British Committee of INC was formed by W Wedderburn (Biographer of AO Hume) in London, a journal India was also started

Safety Valve Controversy
  • Started with WC Banerjee's Statement in 1898
  • Hume was acting under direct commands of Lord Dufferin
  • Dufferrin's idea to have a political organization as a medium between Government and the people, would save administration from outburts
  • This thoery got support from Lala Lajpat Rai, RP Dutt etc.
  • This theory has lost basis after opening of Dufferin's private papers
