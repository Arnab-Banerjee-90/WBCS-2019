Governor-Generals and Viceroys

Governor-Generals of Bengal

Warren Hastings (1772-1785)
  • Became Governor of Bengal in 1772
  • Became Governor-General of Bengal in 1773 via Regulating act of 1773
  • His four councellors were Clavering, Francis, Monson and Barell
  • Termination of dual administration in Bengal 1772
  • Quinquennial settlement of land revenue by method of farming out estates to the highest bidder
  • Rohila war (1774) and annexation of Rohilkhand by Nawab of Oudh with the help of the British
  • Creation of Board of Revenue in 1772
  • Created Diwani and Fauzdari Adalat
  • Codified Hindu and Muslim law
  • Supreme court established at Fort william in 1774, appointed Elijah Impey as the chief justice
  • Established Calcutta/Aliya Madrasah in 1781 for study of Islamic law
  • Made Elijah Impey the Superintendent of the Sadar Diwani Adalat
  • Called The father of judicial reforms in India
  • Stopped annual allowances of ₹ 26 lakh to emperor Shah Alam II
  • Took Allahabad and Kara from him and sold to the Nawab of Awadh
  • Trial of Nand Kumar and his judicial murder in 1775
  • Chait Singh affair (1778), he was Raja of Benaras
  • He was the only Governor-General against whom impeachment proceeding were proceeded
  • Quinquinnellion settlement abandonded in 1777
  • Founded Asiatic Society with Sir William Jones in 1784
  • Conservator of Asiatic Society, helped in translation of Abhigyan Shakuntalam and Gita Govinda

Lord Cornwallis (1786-1793)
  • Europeanization of administrative services
  • Introduction of Civil Services
  • Reforms to purify and improve administration
  • Called The father of Civil Services in India
  • Police system was introduced
  • All officers were responsible to the courts
  • Introduced Permanent Settlement Act or the Zamindari system in Bengal and Bihar in 1793
  • Cornwallis Code → Judiciary Reforms (1793) setting up courts at differnt levels, seperation of revenue administration from judicial administration

Sir John Shore (1793-1798)
  • Battle of Kharda took place between Nizam and Marathas (1795)
  • A regulation prohibiting female infanticide was passed in 1795

Lord Wellesley (1798-1805)
  • Called himself The Tiger of Bengal
  • Applied policy of Subsidiary alliance to achieve British Paramountacy in India
  • Fourth Anglo-Mysore war 1799, resulted in the death of Tipu Sultan and annexation of many parts of Mysore
  • Subsidiary treaty of Bassein (1802)
  • Second Anglo-Maratha war (1803-1805), resulted in the defeat of Sindhia, Bhonsle and Holkar
  • Formation of the Madras Presidency

Sir George Barlow (1805-1807)
  • Second Anglo-Maratha was ended
  • Vellore mutiny of 1806
  • Slave trade abolished in British Empire in 1807

Lord Minto I (1807-1813)
  • Treaty of Amritsar with Ranjit Singh in 1809
  • Rebellion in Travancore

Lord Hastings (1813-1823)
  • Real name Francis Edward Rawdon-Hastings, Hastings was his additional Surname
  • Anglo-Nepal war (1814-1816), Gorkhas accepted Treaty of Saguli
  • Third Maratha War, Maratha power was finally crushed
  • Abolition of Peshwaship
  • Formation of Bombay Presidency in 1818

Lord Armherst (1823-1828)
  • First Burmese war (1824-1826)
  • Barrackpur Mutiny of 1824
  • Capture of Bharatpur 1826

Governor-Generals of India

Lord William Bentick (1828-1835)
  • First Governor-General of India via Charter act of 1833
  • Suppression of child sacrifices and infanticides
  • Suppression of Thugi 1829-1837
  • Abolition of Sati in 1829, and revolt of Khasis
  • Educational reforms on the basis of Macaulay's minute
  • Introduction of English as the official language and the medium of instruction
  • Establishment of the first Medical College in Calcutta (1835)
  • Annexation of Mysore, Coorg, Chachar and Jaintia

Sir Charles Metcalfe (1835-1836)
  • Passed education resolution proposed by Macaulay
  • Abolition of press restrictions Liberator of Indian Press

Lord Auckland (1836-1842)
  • Started construction of GT road from Calcutta to Delhi in 1839
  • First Afgan war (1836-1842). Disaster of British, Auckland recalled
  • In 1838 tripartite treaty between Shah Shuja, Ranjit Singh and the British

Lord Ellenborough (1842-1844)
  • Slavery abolished (1843)
  • Conquest and annexation of Sind (1843) by Cahrles Napier

Lord Hardinge I (1844-1848)
  • Henry Hardinge, 1st Viscount Hardinge
  • Danish possessions sold to the British in 1845
  • First Anglo-Sikh war and the Lahore Treaty in 1846
  • Abolition of female infanticide
  • Supression of the practice of Human sacrifice among the Khonds, Khond rebellion in 1846

Lord Dalhousie (1848-1856)
  • Used his policy Doctrine of Lapse to seize of differnt regions of India
  • Second Sikh war (1848-49), annexation of Punjab
  • Second Anglo-Burma war, annexation of lower Burma
  • Considered as Father of the Electric Telegraph in India
  • O'Shaunessey appointed as the superintendent of Telegraph Department in 1852
  • First Telegraph line from Calcutta to Agra
  • Railway minute of 1853, first railway line from Bombay to Thane
  • Charter act of 1853 was passed
  • Wood's Dispatch in 1854, British assumption of the responsibility of educating the masses
  • Recruitment to Civil Services via competitive examination
  • Post office act of 1854, postage stamps issued for the first time
  • Seperate PWD was established in every province
  • Widow re-marriage act of 1856

Viceroys of India

Lord Canning (1856-1862)
  • Last Governor-General and First viceroy of India after Government Of India Act
  • Sepoy Mutiny occured
  • Income Tax was introduced for the first time in 1858
  • Doctrine of Lapse withdrawn and Indian Penal Code passed in 1859
  • Indigo riots in Bengal
  • Indian Councils Act of 1861 was passed
  • Indain High Court Act of 1861 was passed
  • Universities in Calcutta, Madras and Bombay was established

Lord Elgin I (1862-1863)
  • Wahabi movement supressed

Sir John Lawrence (1864-1869)
  • High Courts were established in Calcutta, Bombay in Madras in 1865
  • War with Bhutan in 1865
  • Punjab Tennency Act was passed

Lord Mayo (1869-1872)
  • Introduced financial de-centralization
  • Established Department of Agriculture and Commerce
  • First Census deon in India in 1872
  • Only Viceroy to be murdered in office by a convict in Andaman in 1872

Lord NorthBrook (1872-1876)
  • Kuka rebellion in 1872
  • Famine in Bihar in 1876

Lord Lytton (1876-1880)
  • Most unpopular viceroy in Indain history
  • Arranged Grand Durbar in Delhi when the country was reeling under famine
  • Royal title act of 1876 was passed
  • Queen Elizabeth was declared Kaiser-I-Hind
  • Passed Arms Act of 1872
  • Vernacular Press Act of 1878
  • Second Afgan War 1878-80
