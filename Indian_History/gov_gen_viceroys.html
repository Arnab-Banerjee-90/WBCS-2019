Governor-Generals and Viceroys

Governor-Generals of Bengal

Warren Hastings (1772-1785)
  • Became Governor of Bengal in 1772
  • Became Governor-General of Bengal in 1773 via Regulating act of 1773
  • His four councellors were Clavering, Francis, Monson and Barell
  • Termination of dual administration in Bengal 1772
  • Quinquennial settlement of land revenue by method of farming out estates to the highest bidder
  • Rohila war (1774) and annexation of Rohilkhand by Nawab of Oudh with the help of the British
  • Creation of Board of Revenue in 1772
  • Created Diwani and Fauzdari Adalat
  • Codified Hindu and Muslim law
  • Supreme court established at Fort william in 1774, appointed Elijah Impey as the chief justice
  • Established Calcutta/Aliya Madrasah in 1781 for study of Islamic law
  • Made Elijah Impey the Superintendent of the Sadar Diwani Adalat
  • Called The father of judicial reforms in India
  • Stopped annual allowances of ₹ 26 lakh to emperor Shah Alam II
  • Took Allahabad and Kara from him and sold to the Nawab of Awadh
  • Trial of Nand Kumar and his judicial murder in 1775
  • Chait Singh affair (1778), he was Raja of Benaras
  • He was the only Governor-General against whom impeachment proceeding were proceeded
  • Quinquinnellion settlement abandonded in 1777
  • Founded Asiatic Society with Sir William Jones in 1784
  • Conservator of Asiatic Society, helped in translation of Abhigyan Shakuntalam and Gita Govinda

Lord Cornwallis (1786-1793)
  • Europeanization of administrative services
  • Introduction of Civil Services
  • Reforms to purify and improve administration
  • Called The father of Civil Services in India
  • Police system was introduced
  • All officers were responsible to the courts
  • Introduced Permanent Settlement Act or the Zamindari system in Bengal and Bihar in 1793
  • Cornwallis Code → Judiciary Reforms (1793) setting up courts at differnt levels, seperation of revenue administration from judicial administration

Sir John Shore (1793-1798)
  • Battle of Kharda took place between Nizam and Marathas (1795)
  • A regulation prohibiting female infanticide was passed in 1795

Lord Wellesley (1798-1805)
  • Called himself The Tiger of Bengal
  • Applied policy of Subsidiary alliance to achieve British Paramountacy in India
  • Fourth Anglo-Mysore war 1799, resulted in the death of Tipu Sultan and annexation of many parts of Mysore
  • Subsidiary treaty of Bassein (1802)
  • Second Anglo-Maratha war (1803-1805), resulted in the defeat of Sindhia, Bhonsle and Holkar
  • Formation of the Madras Presidency

Sir George Barlow (1805-1807)
  • Second Anglo-Maratha was ended
  • Vellore mutiny of 1806
  • Slave trade abolished in British Empire in 1807

Lord Minto I (1807-1813)
  • Treaty of Amritsar with Ranjit Singh in 1809
  • Rebellion in Travancore

Lord Hastings (1813-1823)
  • Anglo-Nepal war (1814-1816), Gorkhas accepted Treaty of Saguli
  • Third Maratha War, Maratha power was finally crushed
  • Abolition of Peshwaship
  • Formation of Bombay Presidency in 1818

Lord Armherst (1823-1828)
  • First Burmese war (1824-1826)
  • Barrackpur Mutiny of 1824
  • Capture of Bharatpur 1826
