The Sultanate of Delhi

Formation of The Sultanate
  • By end of 12th Century Ghori successfully occupied Delhi and started a dynasty of rulers
  • Ghori's Sudden death in 1206 resulted in a scramble for power among Qutubuddin Aibak, Tajuddin Yalduz and Nasiruddin Qubacha
  • Ghias-ud-din Mamud successor of Ghori Acknowledged Aibak as the sovereign ruler
  • Assumption of Power by Aibak in 1206 is regarded as the starting of the Delhi Sultanate

Slave/Ilbari/Mameluq (1206-90)

Qutubuddin Aibak (1206-10)
  • Capital Lahore and later Delhi
  • First Muslim king of India
  • Known as Lakh Baksh
  • Great patron of learning, patronised writes like Hasan-Ul-Nizami and Fakhruddin
  • Tazul Maasir of Hasan Nizami is a work primarily dealing with Aibak
  • He began construction of Quwwat-Ul-Islam, Adhai Din-Ka-Jhopra, and Qutub Minar
  • In 1210 wile playing Chaugan (Polo) he fell off his horse and died
