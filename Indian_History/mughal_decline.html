Later Mughal Emperors

Bahadur Shah I (1707-12)
  • Bahadur Shah I defeated his brother Kam Baksh in the Battle of Jajau in 1707
  • He released Sahu the son of Sambhaji and recognised the independence of Mewar and Marwar
  • He was called Shah-i-Bekhabar
  • He made peace with Guru Govind Singh and allowed the Marathas to collect Sardeshmukhi of the Deccan
  • Led an unsuccessful campaign against Banda Bahadur, he died during the course of this campaign
  • A Dutch representative committee under the leadership of Jashua Ketelar visited his court in 1711

Jahandar Shah (1712-13)
  • Captured the throne by liquidating his brothers with the help of Zulfiqar Khan
  • Zulfikar Khan acted as the de-facto ruler
  • He was dominated by his mistress Lal Kunwar, who is said to have imitated the style of Nur Jahan
  • He granted the Marathas, the Chauth and Sardeshmukhi, but it had to be collected by a Mughal official
  • He was the first Mughal Emperor to be killed in captivity by the Sayyed Brothers
