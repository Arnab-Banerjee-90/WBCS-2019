Tribal and Peasant Uprisings

Tribal Movements
Movements Location Leaders Cause
Fakir and Sanyasi rebellion (1750-) Bengal Manju Shah Fakir Economic disterss caused due to continuous wars,famiens and British exploitation
Pagal Panthis (1825-35) Bengal Karam Shah, Tipu Shah (Hajing and Garo tribes) Against rent hike of zamindars
Ahom Revolt (1828-33) Assam Gomdhar Konwar Incorporation of Ahom territories into EIC's dominion
Kol Mutiny (1831-32) Ranchi, Singbhum, Hazaribagh Buddhu Bhagat Transfer of land form Kol headmen to outsiders like Muslim and Sikh farmers
Khasi Uprising (1829-33) Garo, Khasi, Jayantia hills in Meghalaya Tirath Singh British wanted to build a road connecting Brahmaputra valley to Sylhet, this led to infiltration of foreign population into their land
Santhal Rebellion (1855-56) Santhal areas of Bengal, Bihar and Orissa Sidhu and Kanhu Exploitaion of moneylenders and colonial administration
Munda Uprising (1899-1900) Ranchi and Chotanagpur Birsa Munda Feudal zamindari

Peasant Uprisings
Movement Location Leaders Cause
Indigo Revolt (1860) Nadia district of Bengal Digambar Biswas, Bishnu Biswas, Harish Chandra Chatterjee
  • Peasants forced to grow indigo
  • Dinabandhu Mitra wrote about this revolt in his play called Neel Darpan, translated into English by Madhusudhan Dutta
Poona Sarvajanik Sabha (1870) Puna MG Ranade To popularise peasants legal rights
Pabna Agrarian Uprising (1873) Pabna district of Bengal Shah Chandra Roy, Shambhu Patel Against opression of pesants by zamindars, Bengal tenency act of 1885 passed
Ramosi Uprising (1877-87) Parts of Andhra pradesh and Maharashtra Vasudev Balwant Phadke Against British failure to take up anti-famine measure
Bijolia Movement (1905,13,16,27) Rajasthan Sitaram Das, Vijay Pathak Singh Impositions of 86 different cases on peasants
