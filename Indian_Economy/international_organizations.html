International Economic Organizations

Bretton Woods Conference

Cause
  • To agree upon a series of new rules for Post WWII International Monetary System

Accomplishment
  • Formation of IMF
  • Formation of IRDB or World Bank

International Monitary Fund

Formation
  • Conceived on 22nd July 1944
  • Established on 27th Dec 1945

Purpose
  • Oversee and maintain an orderly system of payments and receipts between nations

Members
  • Originally had 45 members
  • At present it has 189 members
  • India was one of the founding memebrs

Headquarters
  • Washington DC

Quotas
  • Determines the amount of Foreign Exchange a member can borrow
  • Quotas also determines the Voting power of the member on IMF policy matters
  • USA has the largest quota followed by Japan and China
  • India having quota share of 2.76% is eighth

SDR
  • Special Drawing Rights
  • Created in 1969
  • Called Paper Gold
  • Supplementary foreign exchange reserve assets
  • Represents claim to currency held by the members
  • Value is determined by the weighted currency basket of $ £ ¥ € and Chinese Yuan

Indian Governor
  • Finance Minister is the ex-officio governor of the Board of Governors
  • Governor of the RBI is India's alternate Governor

World Bank

Formation
  • Formed in 1944 via Bretton Woods Conference
  • Came to formal existance on 27th December 1945

Members
  • Currently 189 members

Purpose
  • Promote Economic and social progress in developing countries
  • Provides Loans to developing countries

Headquarters
  • Washington DC

World Bank Groups

IBRD
  • International Bank for Reconstruction and Development
  • Established in 1944
  • Originally aimed to helping Europe recover from WWII
  • Works with middle-income and credit worthy poorer countries to promote sustainable, job-creating growth
  • 189 member countries

IFC
  • International Finance Commission
  • Established in 1956
  • Aim to providing sustainable private sector growth
  • 184 member countries
  • Headquartered in Washington DC

IDA
  • International Development Association
  • Established in 1960
  • Known as Soft Loan Window of the World Bank

ICSID
  • International Center for Settlement of Investment Disputes
  • Established in 1966
  • To facilitate the conciliation and arbitration of investment disputes

MIGA
  • Multilateral Investment Guarantee Agency
  • Founded in 1988
  • To promote FDI in developing countries

World Trade Organisation

Formation
  • Constituted on 1st Jan 1995, under the Marrakesh Agreement
  • Took the place of GATT (Genral Agreement on Trade and Tariff)
  • GATT was an informal organisation which regulated world trade since 1948

Headquarters
  • Geneva

Members
  • Currently has 164 members
  • Afganisthan is the 164th member

Functions
  • Oversee, implement the WTO agreements
  • Provide a forum for negotiations
  • Administration and operation of multilateral and bilateral agreements of World trade

WTO Ministrial Conferences
Conference Year Place Policies
1st 1996 Singapore Singapore Issue, i.e transparency in government procurement, facilitaion etc
2nd 1998 Geneva Swiss formula, relates to NAMA (Non agricultural market access)
4th 2001 Doha DDA , Doha Development Agenda to enhance equitable participation of poor countries

Key Agreements of WTO

GATT
  • General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade

GATS
  • General Agreement on Trade in Services
  • Banks, Insurance, Telecomm, Tour & Hotel chains come under it

TRIPS
  • Trade related aspects of Intellectual Property Rights
  • Product and Public patents
  • It gave developing countries 10 years to enact laws to protect intellectual property
  • India enacted its Patent act in 2005 conforming to the agreement

NAMA
  • Non-Agricultural Market Access
  • Refers to all products not covered by the agreement on agriculture

WTO Boxes
  • Subsidies are identified by boxes
  • Red Box Forbidden
  • Amber Slowed down
  • Green Permitted
